## [1.3.5] - 2026-05-04

### Fixed
- **Outlier robustness filter for all solar learning paths.** A single meter glitch or stale-read (e.g., a 100x nominal impact sample) previously corrupted the live Tobit and NLMS coefficients for up to ~150 hours because the outlier persisted in the sliding window. A new per-unit pre-pass filter now rejects these anomalies based on a Median Absolute Deviation (MAD) of recent residuals. The filter dynamically calculates a robust standard deviation (sigma) from the last 50 samples and drops residuals exceeding `5 × sigma`. 
  - **Comprehensive coverage:** Protects NLMS, Tobit live, inequality (shutdown hours), and batch-fit learning paths.
  - **Shift vs. Glitch recovery:** A two-tier mechanism distinguishes isolated glitches from genuine physical shifts (e.g., new heat pump installed). If 10 consecutive rejected samples show a narrow internal spread, they are automatically promoted to form the new baseline.
  - **Warm-up and small-load protection:** Before enough samples exist for MAD, a hard physical cap (10 kWh) drops obvious glitches. The robust sigma also enforces a per-entity floor (`0.05 × min_base`), scaling the sensitivity cleanly from tiny 10W wine-cellar loads up to large 1kW+ heat pumps.
  - **Prior-free sanity check:** An independent fallback `|actual - expected| > 10 × expected` catches massive glitches even when the current learned coefficient is heavily biased. The `diagnose_solar` and `apply_implied_coefficient` paths apply only this prior-free sanity check (not the MAD baseline) so the "commit diagnose verbatim" contract is preserved.
- **Cooling cold-start gate extended past hour 4 via solar `learned` flag.**  The cooling cold-start fix below releases when the cooling base bucket has at least one entry (`LEARNING_BUFFER_THRESHOLD = 4` cooling-mode hour boundaries).  Field evidence on the maintainer install (2026-05-01, VP-Stue 6.5-hour cooling stretch) shows the solar coefficient typically takes much longer than 4 hours to be overwritten by the solar learner — NLMS / cold-start solver / inequality / batch-fit all gate on `expected_unit_base ≥ nlms_threshold`, which an idling cooling room may not satisfy for many hours after the base learner has released.  Between hour 5 and the first solar-learner write, the migration-seeded coefficient continues to drive phantom additive demand at reduced magnitude.  Fix adds a second prong to `_is_cooling_solar_cold_start`: every solar-learner write in `_update_unit_solar_coefficient` now stamps `learned: True` on the regime dict, and the cold-start gate also returns `True` when the cooling regime lacks the flag.  Migration-seeded cooling coefficients (verbatim copies of the heating coefficient from `_migrate_v3_to_v4`) carry no flag and read as unlearned, exactly the case we want to gate.  Gate releases on the first solar-learner write to the cooling regime.  No `STORAGE_VERSION` bump — the flag is additive within the existing nested dict shape, and `.get("learned", False)` defaulting handles legacy storage as if the flag were `False` (self-healing on first write, no migration required).  An earlier draft of this fix added a v5→v6 migration with a vector-equality heuristic to backfill the flag; the heuristic was rejected because coefficients are stored rounded to 5 decimals and a legitimately-learned cooling coefficient that differs from heating by exactly one quantization step would be misclassified as unlearned.
- **`thermodynamic_gross_kwh` solar sign is now mode-aware per unit.**  Pre-fix, the solar adjustment used `avg_temp >= balance_point` as a house-aggregate proxy for "is the building cooling?".  This was wrong on (1) mixed-mode hours where some units were heating and others cooling within the same hour, and (2) cooling-near-BP / heating-above-BP hours where unit mode and outdoor temperature disagreed.  Field evidence (maintainer install 2026-05-01): `thermodynamic_gross_kwh` went negative (-0.137 to -0.287 kWh) during cooling-mode hours because the temp-vs-BP gate flipped the solar sign for the entire house, including any heating units active in the same hour.  Fix in `hourly_processor` replaces the proxy with per-unit-mode attribution via `solar_heating_applied_kwh − solar_cooling_applied_kwh` from `calculate_total_power`'s breakdown — these fields are already per-unit-mode aggregates of this hour's applied solar.  The signed mode-share fraction is multiplied with the battery-smoothed scalar so lagged thermal-mass release still adjusts gross with the correct sign.  Fallback for hours with no applied solar but residual battery (post-sunset thermal mass): classify by active unit modes — residual mass benefits heating, hurts cooling.  Mixed or all-OFF/DHW: drop the adjustment (cannot apportion).  Display sensors (`thermodynamic_gross_today_kwh`, `last_hour_thermodynamic_gross_kwh`) and downstream consumers (daily aggregation, guest-mode forecast accuracy) inherit the corrected sign automatically.
- **Cooling cold-start no longer adds phantom solar demand on first cooling-mode use.**  When an entity switches to cooling mode for the first time after upgrading from 1.3.3, the cooling solar coefficient stored in `_solar_coefficients_per_unit[entity]["cooling"]` is the migration-seeded value from `_migrate_v3_to_v4` (which wrapped the pre-1.3.4 flat coefficient as both heating AND cooling regimes verbatim, to preserve manually-set values).  This seeded coefficient was never actually learned from cooling-mode data — it's the heating-time value frozen at migration.  Combined with `solar.calculate_saturation`'s cooling-mode behaviour (solar potential adds to demand additively, no saturation cap because "sun heats the room → cooling needs more energy"), the seeded coefficient produced phantom additive demand on every cooling-mode hour, accumulating to several kWh of fictitious expected load over a sunny afternoon.  Field evidence (maintainer install): a Toshiba VP-Stue with seeded cooling coefficient `{s:0.23, e:0.22, w:0.42}` switched to cooling for ~6.5 hours produced ~5 kWh excess `expected_today_kwh` accumulation that persisted after switching back to heating (the hourly log entries are frozen at boundary write time).  Read-side fix in `statistics.calculate_total_power` and `_calculate_fallback_projection`: cooling-mode prediction skips the solar contribution entirely when the entity is in cooling cold-start (no `"cooling"` sub-key in any temp_key of `correlation_data_per_unit[entity]`).  Cooling base prediction is also 0 in cold-start (existing semantic), so net cooling-mode prediction is 0 — matching the documented "warm-up period returns 0" behaviour for the cooling base bucket.  Gate releases automatically once the cooling base bucket has at least one entry (`LEARNING_BUFFER_THRESHOLD = 4` cooling-mode hour boundaries), at which point cooling solar applies normally.  Limitation: this fix addresses the cold-start phase only.  After 4+ cooling-mode hours the gate releases, but the solar coefficient is still migration-seeded until enough cooling-mode samples accumulate for the solar learner's gates to fire — phantom demand persists at reduced magnitude during this transient.  Users who want immediate correction can run `reset_solar_learning entity_id: <entity> mode: cooling` to reset the seeded coefficient to defaults; cold-start solver rebuilds from cooling-mode data.
- **Live Tobit excludes MPC-managed sensors even when allow-listed.**  `experimental_tobit_live_learner` activation gate previously honored only the user's `tobit_live_entities` allow-list.  Track C / MPC-managed sensors (those with a `WeightedSmear(use_synthetic=True)` strategy) accidentally added to the allow-list would have fed Tobit load-shifted samples — the HP runs hard at night and idles during sun under MPC scheduling, decoupled from solar potential.  Tobit fitting against such samples produces non-physical coefficients.  Mirrors the `weighted_smear_excluded` skip path that `batch_fit_solar_coefficients` has applied since 1.3.4; now consistent across all paths.  Track C users continue to use `apply_implied_coefficient` (path 5) for MPC-managed sensors, which is the documented mechanism.  Defense-in-depth — opt-in misconfiguration cannot accidentally feed Tobit MPC data.
- **Solar-shutdown detection now flags parasitic standby on low-base hours.**  Pre-fix, the gate ordering in `detect_solar_shutdown_entities` checked the per-unit `base ≥ unit_min_base` eligibility BEFORE the absolute parasitic-floor check (`actual < SOLAR_SHUTDOWN_ACTUAL_FLOOR`).  On low-modulation heat pumps in mild weather, this missed an entire class of "compressor physically off, sensor reading ~12 Wh standby" hours: base was below the per-unit threshold (~0.169 kWh on a Toshiba VP at 12-16°C outdoor), so the entity was skipped before the absolute floor (0.03 kWh) ever applied — and the hour fell through to the modulating-regime learning paths with `actual_impact ≈ 0.07 kWh ("86% of base disappeared")`, inflating the solar coefficient on what was physically a fully-off compressor.  Fix moves the absolute parasitic-floor check before the eligibility gate so any hour with a positive base and `actual < SOLAR_SHUTDOWN_ACTUAL_FLOOR` is flagged shutdown regardless of base level.  Offline-sensor case (base = 0) remains unaffected.  Field evidence on the maintainer install: 10 of 44 sun hours over 10 days were borderline-misclassified pre-fix; post-fix they correctly route to the inequality learner.  Concrete impact on existing installs: any unit running at low-modulation in mild weather will see slightly fewer modulating-regime hours and slightly more shutdown-regime hours after upgrade.  NLMS will stop reinforcing the upward bias on those misclassified hours (newly-flagged-shutdown samples are excluded from NLMS rather than pushed downward, so the existing inflated value drops only as the remaining correctly-classified modulating hours pull it down — magnitude per install depends on the modulating-hour SNR distribution and the size of the prior bias).  This is a correction, not a regression — the previous values were inflated.

- **Global base EMA no longer learns in the wrong direction on solar-saturated hours.**  When the per-unit applied solar exceeded the global base (`solar_normalization_delta ≥ global_base`), the global net clipped to zero (`max(0, base − solar) = 0`) and `expected_kwh` was logged as 0.  The base-bucket EMA still ran, using the raw solar-reduced actual as its target — a value guaranteed to be below the bucket, pulling the base downward on exactly the hours where the dark-sky demand is hardest to observe.  Field evidence (maintainer install, 12°C shoulder bucket, 2026-05-03 hour 16): `global_base = 0.201 kWh`, `solar_normalization_delta = 0.379 kWh`, `actual = 0.199 kWh`, `thermodynamic_gross = 0.444 kWh` — EMA stepped from 0.201 → 0.200 against true dark-sky demand of ~0.444 kWh.  At shoulder temperatures (10–14°C) in spring, nearly all hours in the bucket are daytime solar-affected hours — the "dark hours dominate" property of SNR weighting breaks down when dark hours at that temperature are structurally absent from the season.  Fix: the EMA update is suppressed when `(global_base − solar_normalization_delta) < GLOBAL_BASE_SATURATION_SKIP_KWH` (default 0.0, i.e., exactly when global net clips to zero).  Dark hours at the same temperature continue to correct the bucket.  The new constant `GLOBAL_BASE_SATURATION_SKIP_KWH` in `const.py` can be raised to a small positive value (e.g. `0.010`, ≈ 10 W) to also suppress near-saturated hours where the learning signal is similarly unreliable.  Cold-start buffer (`base_expected_kwh == 0`) is unaffected — the gate only fires once the bucket is initialised.  Hours suppressed by this gate are reported as `learning_status: "skipped_global_saturation"` in the hourly log.

- **Global base EMA gains a one-sided dark-equivalent floor for shoulder-temperature buckets.**  The saturation-skip above stops the wrong-direction step on fully-clipped hours, but leaves a structural problem on partially-saturated hours: at shoulder temperatures (10–14°C) in spring/autumn, the temperature bucket is populated almost exclusively by sunny daytime hours — nighttime temperatures sit outside the band — so SNR weighting cannot recover.  Each sunny hour pulls the bucket toward the solar-reduced actual; SNR attenuates the step but does not reverse direction.  Over a season the bucket drifts toward roughly half of true dark-sky demand.  Fix: when the existing bucket sits below `actual + solar_normalization_delta` AND at least one solar coefficient in an active learning regime has been marked `learned`, the EMA target is the dark-equivalent value; otherwise the legacy raw-actual EMA applies.  The lift is one-sided (never pulls down) and bounded by `dark_target`, so an overestimated solar coefficient inflates the target but cannot drive runaway growth — dark and SNR-attenuated hours own the downward path and pull the bucket back if it overshoots.  The plausibility gate is loose (any single learned coefficient unlocks the lift) but defends day-1 fresh-install / post-reset fleets where every coefficient is still seeded: in that state a saturated shoulder-bucket hour would have no anchor pulling `dark_target` toward truth, so the lift is suppressed and the saturation-skip gate from #929 remains reachable, freezing the bucket until enough modulating-regime hours produce a learned coefficient.  Once the gate is open, the lift takes precedence over the saturation skip — the under-converged shoulder-bucket case is exactly what the floor was added to fix.  Same one-sided semantic applied symmetrically in the retrain path (`learn_from_historical_import`).

### Added
- **`seed_live_window` parameter on `batch_fit_solar` service.**  When the user upgrades through a classification change (such as the parasitic-floor gate fix above) or a learning-pipeline change in general, the live Tobit sliding window can hold samples that no longer match current model semantics.  Pre-existing options were `reset_solar_learning` (wipes everything, accepts a 25-day cold-start during which Tobit can't write) or do-nothing (window keeps producing biased coefficients).  The new opt-in `seed_live_window: true` parameter on `batch_fit_solar` rebuilds the live Tobit window in-place from the same samples used in the batch fit, sets the slot's `solar_model_version` tag, and writes the batch-fit's Newton-iteration diagnostics as `last_step`.  Critically, the batch-fit sample-collection path with `for_tobit=True` reclassifies shutdown samples under CURRENT detection rules (re-running `detect_solar_shutdown_entities` per log entry) instead of trusting the persisted `solar_dominant_entities` flag — without this, log entries written before a classification change would carry their old (possibly buggy) flags through to the new window, defeating the migration intent.  Tobit's next qualifying hour finds a populated, current-version window and refines from there — no cold-start.  Default off; opt-in only via service-call argument.  Combines naturally with `dry_run: true` for preview (window not seeded in dry-run) and `entity_id` filter for scoped migration.  Recommended after applying the shutdown-gate fix above: `service: heating_analytics.batch_fit_solar, data: {entity_id: <vp>, days_back: 30, seed_live_window: true}` migrates one entity's Tobit state without disturbing other learning paths; the seed replaces the live window contents directly so the corrected coefficient is in effect immediately.

### Changed
- **`batch_fit_solar` service now uses Tobit MLE instead of unconstrained LS.** The on-demand `heating_analytics.batch_fit_solar` service replaces its joint 3×3 normal-equations solver with the Tobit projected-Newton MLE introduced earlier in this release for diagnose-only shadow analysis.  Saturation-clipped samples (HP fully off because the room got warm) are no longer dropped — they are kept as right-censored data with threshold `T_i = BATCH_FIT_SATURATION_RATIO × base_i` and contribute slope information to the fit via the Mills-ratio likelihood term.  Field evidence (maintainer install diagnose 2026-04-27): on the Toshiba VP west-facing window, the Tobit estimator closes 97 % of the gap to physical ground truth (W coefficient: 0.78 vs ground truth ≥ 0.80, where the LS form returned 0.14 and inequality returned 0.39).  Live wiring lowest-risk because the service is opt-in — users explicitly invoke it.  Damping (α=0.3) blend on prior coefficient stays as a safety net; `dry_run` flag stays.  Sample-size gate flips from `BATCH_FIT_MIN_SAMPLES = 30` (raw count) to two-pronged Tobit gates: `|U| ≥ TOBIT_MIN_UNCENSORED (20)` pre-fit (σ identifiability) and `n_eff = |U| + Σ_C λ(q)(λ(q)−q) ≥ TOBIT_MIN_NEFF (40)` post-fit (slope identifiability).  `BATCH_FIT_MIN_SAMPLES` constant removed; `_solve_batch_fit_normal_equations` retained for the cold-start solver in `_learn_unit_solar_coefficient` (cold-start sees 4 samples, below Tobit's σ-identifiability floor).  Per-regime diagnostic dict gains `tobit_diagnostics` block (`iterations`, `converged`, `sigma`, `n_uncensored`, `n_censored`, `censored_fraction`, `n_eff`, `log_likelihood`); `coefficient_before` / `coefficient_after` / `damping_applied` / `applied` / `dry_run` fields are unchanged.  `skip_reason` vocabulary updates: `insufficient_samples` → `insufficient_uncensored` (pre-fit gate), new `insufficient_effective_samples` (post-fit n_eff gate), new `did_not_converge` (Newton failed in 30 iter), `degenerate_fit` → `warm_start_failed` (LS warm-start returned None).  Tobit also drops the noise-floor `unit_threshold` gate that LS used (live NLMS still applies it) — Tobit needs the mild-weather low-base shoulder hours to characterise σ at the noise floor.  Shutdown samples are still excluded (owned by the inequality learner — modulating-regime fit only).  Tobit reduces to OLS exactly when no rows are saturated, so installs without saturation see no fit-quality regression vs the pre-stage-2 form.  Falsification criteria for the next stage (replacing the live NLMS per-hour learner with a Tobit-aware variant) are tracked separately and require additional field evidence before being lifted to a default.

### Added
- **Tobit live coefficient learner — default-on with automatic plausibility-gate v2 (storage v5).** A running sliding-window Tobit MLE replaces the per-hour NLMS update on entities where an automatic plausibility-gate identifies real solar response.  Pre-1.3.5 design framing was "experimental opt-in"; field evidence and product reasoning during the 1.3.5 development window concluded that manual allow-list as the primary discriminator is wrong for a self-host integration's distribution model — users will not opt-in per-entity.  The release that adds the live learner therefore also ships it default-on, with the discrimination work moved into automatic per-entity detection.

  **Plausibility-gate v2 — three coupled checks.** After each Tobit Newton iteration succeeds on the heating regime, three checks decide whether the coefficient is written:

  1. **Magnitude floor.** An unconstrained-OLS sub-fit on the uncensored samples in the sliding window must show at least one direction (S, E, W) with `|coeff| ≥ PLAUSIBILITY_MIN_OLS_MAX_DIRECTION = 0.10`.  Heat-pump-modulating-style entities show clear uncensored slope in at least one direction; entities with no real solar response have OLS slopes near zero in all directions and their Tobit fit would be censoring-pattern-driven, producing non-physical coefficients — so the write is skipped and NLMS retains authority.  A secondary floor (`PLAUSIBILITY_MIN_TOBIT_MAGNITUDE = 0.05`) skips the check when the Tobit fit is itself near-zero (zero coefficients are a no-op write).  Magnitude ratio (`|OLS|/|Tobit|`) was tried first and rejected because Tobit's amplification factor is similar across legitimate VPs and tiny non-VP loads — the very property that makes Tobit valuable on heavily-censored installs also makes the ratio uninformative.  Threshold 0.10 sits cleanly between calibrated legitimate-VP OLS-max (0.12-0.33 across maintainer install) and small-load OLS-max (≤ 0.04) — bump-rule documented in `const.py` for revision after multi-install evidence accumulates.

  2. **Direction agreement.** Tobit's projected-Newton active-set can pin a direction at zero from a wrong warm-start (e.g. NLMS cold-started with `{s: 1.0, e: 0, w: 0}` while real signal is W-dominant), producing a wrong-direction magnitude that satisfies the magnitude floor because OLS-on-uncensored still correctly identifies the real direction.  Cosine similarity between the Tobit and OLS coefficient vectors must clear `PLAUSIBILITY_MIN_DIRECTION_COSINE = 0.5` (≈ 60° tolerance).  Catches the warm-start direction-pinning failure that magnitude-only gating cannot see.

  3. **Cooling regime skips both checks.** Cooling has no upper-saturation analog (`is_censored` is heating-only), so every cooling sample is uncensored and Tobit reduces to OLS exactly.  Plausibility on cooling would degenerate to "block when `0.05 < tobit_max < 0.10`" — exactly the band where small-AC-install cooling coefficients naturally converge.  Skipping plausibility on cooling avoids ~50 % structural false-positive on cooling-active installs while keeping the protection for the heating regime where censoring is the actual concern.

  **General step-size limiter.** Every Tobit Newton step is rate-limited so no direction can change by more than `PLAUSIBILITY_RATE_LIMIT_FRACTION = 0.30` of the prior coefficient's maximum component in one hour.  Triggers whenever any direction's proposed step exceeds the cap, regardless of whether the entity was just unblocked or has fast-changing data.  Worst case (NLMS-converged 0.55 → Tobit-fit 1.65 = 200 % jump): converges over ~5 hours (0.715 → 0.929 → 1.207 → 1.569 → 1.65) instead of one 200 %-step that would surface as a sudden deviation-sensor and forecast discontinuity.  Skipped on cold-start (no prior to cushion against).

  **Newton convergence-basin fix (LS-fallback).** Tobit's projected-Newton on `(c, log σ)` has a narrow local convergence basin — sweep-verified at approximately ±10 % of the true coefficient value at typical heating-pump censoring rates (basin width depends on c, σ, and α; widens to ~50 % at small c with no censoring, narrows toward zero as α → 100 %).  Outside the basin, the line search exhausts its budget and the solver returns `did_not_converge`.  Before this fix, Tobit warm-started from a biased NLMS-converged coefficient (e.g. NLMS=0.55 on a heavy-saturation entity where true=1.65) failed Newton on every hour; the live coefficient was never written, NLMS continued writing the biased value, and the system never converged to truth — defeating the entire Stage-3 design.

  The fix is **LS-fallback retry** in `_update_unit_tobit_live`: when the warm-start Newton fails to converge for any reason (typically `line_search_failed` from σ over-correction, but also `max_iter` and `singular_step` on noisier real-world data), retry once with `coeff_init=None` (in-solver LS warm-start), which has its own well-behaved convergence basin determined by the data rather than the prior, and converges reliably (~4 iterations to truth) on any input the original Newton would have rejected.  Combined with the rate-limiter, a Toshiba-class 0.55→1.65 transition now converges over 5 hours in production exactly as the rate-limit was designed for.

  A coupled but secondary fix (**σ-init from LS residuals** in `_solve_tobit_3d`) seeds σ from the LS-fit residuals instead of from the warm-start coefficient's residuals.  Without it, biased c → inflated SSE → σ ≈ 0.5–1.0 instead of true ≈ 0.1 → Newton over-corrects σ on iter 1 and bails immediately.  This fix does NOT widen the convergence basin (verified by direct pre-fix vs post-fix sweep — basin boundaries are identical); it only extends Newton's iteration count before line-search-failure (1 → ~5 iterations on biased priors), giving better diagnose visibility into the failure trajectory and improving σ at convergence on near-basin priors that succeed.  Defense-in-depth, not the load-bearing fix.

  New production-realistic test `test_production_tobit_converges_from_biased_warmstart` pins the trajectory using the actual solver (not a fake-converging mock); the test would fail under any pre-fix variant where the biased warm-start cannot escape via LS-fallback.

  **Logging — transition vs continuation.** The first plausibility block on an (entity, regime) emits at INFO so multi-install evidence collects passively and the maintainer can spot novel discrimination patterns.  Subsequent blocks while the entity remains in the blocked state emit at DEBUG to avoid log-noise on installs with multiple noise-load sensors.  Returns to INFO on the gate clearing (transition logging on both directions).

  **Scope semantic.** `tobit_live_entities` changes from "opt-in list" (must contain entity_id) to "scope override" — empty list = auto-mode (plausibility-gate decides per entity), non-empty list = scope-restrict to listed entities only (plausibility still applies within those — explicit scope-listing does NOT bypass the noise-load filter; the list is scope, not safety override).  Existing maintainer installs with non-empty lists keep the scope behaviour; new users on default-on get auto-mode without configuration.  Service descriptions in `services.yaml` and the `set_*` handler docstrings have been rewritten to match.

  **Default-on migration.** An additive `_tobit_default_applied: bool` storage marker promotes the master flag to `True` on first load (whether fresh install or upgrade from 1.3.4), then stamps itself so subsequent loads respect whatever value the user has set.  Both Tobit service handlers also stamp the marker so an explicit user-disable is durable across restart even if the load-time flip happens to land between save cycles or fails to commit.  The JSON-backup restore path (`async_restore_data`) now runs the v4→v5 migration and applies the same default-on logic, so backups from 1.3.4 restore on 1.3.5+ converge to the same end state as a fresh install rather than inheriting in-session marker state.  No STORAGE_VERSION bump — the marker is additive and idempotent.

  **Diagnose surface.** `live_tobit_state` per-unit block now carries `tobit_routed_to_live` (effective on/off for this entity at this hour given all gates) and `scope_mode` (`"auto"` or `"override"`).  The legacy `allow_listed` field is preserved for backward compatibility but now reports the routed state — pre-1.3.5 it would have reported `entity_id in tobit_live_entities` which is misleading in auto-mode (always False even when Tobit is writing).  New consumers should read `tobit_routed_to_live`.  New `last_step_skip_reason` values: `plausibility_no_uncensored_signal` (magnitude check failed) and `plausibility_direction_mismatch` (cosine check failed).

  **Disabling.** Users who want pre-1.3.5 NLMS-only behaviour can disable via `set_experimental_tobit_live_learner enabled: false`; the marker ensures the disable persists across restart.  The MPC-managed-sensor exclusion (`WeightedSmear(use_synthetic=True)`) remains as defense-in-depth — accidentally scope-listing an MPC sensor still routes to NLMS regardless of plausibility.  Inequality-learner retirement is gated on multi-install evidence and tracked as a follow-up.
- **Tobit MLE estimator surfaced in `diagnose_solar` (shadow-only, stage 0+1).** A new per-unit field `implied_coefficient_tobit_30d` (with companion `tobit_diagnostics` sub-dict reporting iterations, convergence flag, sigma, censored fraction, effective sample size, and skip reason) sits alongside the existing `implied_coefficient_30d` (unconstrained LS dropping saturated rows), `implied_coefficient_30d_no_shutdown`, and `implied_coefficient_inequality` (lower-bound replay).  Tobit is a Type-I right-censored Gaussian regression: saturation-clipped samples (HP fully off because the room got warm) are kept as right-censored data with threshold `T_i = BATCH_FIT_SATURATION_RATIO × base_i` instead of being dropped.  Field evidence motivating the work: maintainer's Toshiba west-facing window shows all three existing estimators biased downward versus physical ground truth (~22%, 55%, 82% under for current_coefficient, inequality, and implied_30d respectively); Mitsubishi 2.etasje has `saturation_pct = 36.4%` so over a third of qualifying hours carry no slope information for batch_fit.  Tobit's Mills-ratio likelihood term recovers slope information from the censoring point itself.  Implementation: `_solve_tobit_3d` runs projected-Newton iterations on `(c_S, c_E, c_W, log σ)` with active-set non-negativity (invariant #4), trust-region clip on `q = (T − c·s)/σ ≥ -5` for Mills-ratio numerical stability (Greene 2003 §17.3), backtracking line search, Tikhonov regulariser on the reduced Hessian for collinear or boundary cases, and a warm-start from the existing 3×3 LS solver (uncensored rows only).  `compute_tobit_for_diagnose` reuses `_collect_batch_fit_samples` with a new `for_tobit=True` flag that keeps saturated rows tagged in a censored mask and excludes shutdown rows (Tobit handles modulating-regime censoring while the inequality learner retains its slow-thermal-mass-aware role for shutdown).  Sample-size gate is `|U| ≥ TOBIT_MIN_UNCENSORED (20) AND n_eff ≥ TOBIT_MIN_NEFF (40)` where `n_eff = |U| + Σ_C λ(q)(λ(q)−q)`.  No production wiring — the existing NLMS / inequality / batch_fit paths are untouched, all live state behaviour is bit-identical.  Stage 1 evidence (running shadow over real installs for 2 weeks) gates promotion to stage 2 (replacing `batch_fit_solar_coefficients` solver with Tobit) and beyond.  Tradeoffs accepted at this stage: vanilla Gauss-Tobit — the test suite includes a 90/10 N(0,σ²)/N(0,9σ²) mixture residual case asserting bias ≤ 30%, with falsification at >10% triggering escalation to Powell CLAD; vanilla within-regime treatment accepting 5-15% residual bias from heterogeneous E[1/COP] until field evidence motivates temp-stratification or a window-inventory Bayesian prior.

## [1.3.4] - 2026-05-01

### Changed
- **`climate_sync` blueprint v2 — asymmetric off-handling and corrected active-mode routing.** Three behaviour fixes in the climate-state-sync blueprint:
  1. **Standard `off` no longer flips the mode helper to `off`.**  Pre-v2, any climate state outside `heat`/`cool` was silently mapped to `off`, which canceled Heating Analytics learning for the duration — even when the off was transient (automation pause, modulation, brief user toggle).  v2 reserves `MODE_OFF` for units the user explicitly disables (matches the convention already documented in `heat_pump_mode_sync.yaml`); standard climate=off now preserves the current mode helper.  Guest mode keeps the climate=off → mode=off mapping (guests genuinely leave; off is the right state for guest units).
  2. **`auto` and `heat_cool` now route to `heating`.**  Pre-v2 they fell into the off default, mislabelling active heating as off.  These are active modes where the climate device picks heat/cool based on room temperature; for installations using this integration (predominantly heating-focused), heating is the right routing.
  3. **`dry` and `fan_only` now preserve.**  Pre-v2 they were silently set to off; v2 leaves the mode helper untouched and lets per-unit base learning absorb whatever modest consumption appears.

  Migration: most users need to do nothing — the new behaviour is strictly more conservative and fixes incorrect routing without breaking the heat/cool happy path.  Users who relied on `climate=off → mode=off` for standard units must now set the select helper to `off` directly via the UI (explicit user intent, matches the reserved meaning of `MODE_OFF`).  Full upgrade notes in `blueprints/MIGRATION.md`.

### Added
- **Apply implied solar coefficient (`apply_implied_coefficient` service).** A new on-demand service that writes `diagnose_solar`'s implied LS-fit into the live solar coefficient for one (unit, mode) regime, with a per-direction stability guard. Bridges the Track C / MPC catch-22 where the online learners (NLMS, inequality, batch_fit_solar) all skip MPC-managed sensors because no coherent dark-equivalent baseline exists in pure-MPC data — leaving those installations stuck on default coefficients with no automatic learning path. The diagnose implied LS fit *is* a usable coefficient for these installations, but until now there was no automated way to commit it. Per-direction stability guard: each of S, E, W is evaluated across the diagnose stability windows; a direction with sign-flip OR `max/min spread > APPLY_IMPLIED_MAX_SPREAD` (default 3.0) is "noise-dominated" and gets skipped (current value preserved); stable directions are written. Near-zero consensus across windows is treated as stable (writes 0.0, clears stale priors). The `force` flag overrides the per-direction guard; the `dry_run` flag runs the analysis but writes nothing. The `days_back` parameter (default 30) restricts the input window — recommended after a retrain or upgrade so the fit doesn't absorb data from before the model state changed; users can also compare `days_back: 7` vs `days_back: 30` (both with `dry_run: true`) to spot-check whether recent data agrees with the longer window. Requires at least `APPLY_IMPLIED_MIN_QUALIFYING_HOURS` (30) qualifying samples. Mode parameter routes to the right regime: `heating` (default), `cooling`, or guest variants; OFF / DHW raise — no learnable solar regime exists for those modes. Fifth coefficient signal path alongside NLMS (drift), inequality (shutdown-edge), cold-start (bootstrap), and batch fit (mild-weather catch-22); ownership boundaries documented in `CLAUDE.md` Solar Model section. Use case: a Track C install runs `diagnose_solar`, sees that implied is stable across windows for S and E but unstable for W (typical west-facing-poor installations), and runs `apply_implied_coefficient` to commit S+E without polluting W with noise.
- **Periodic batch-fit for solar coefficients (`batch_fit_solar` service).** A new on-demand service runs an offline joint least-squares fit per (unit, mode) regime over the modulating-regime hourly log. Bridges the mild-weather catch-22 where the online learners (NLMS + the 1.3.3 inequality path) both produce zero net signal because expected base demand is near zero — typically west-facing rooms whose solar peak coincides with the daily temperature maximum, leaving every NLMS sample with `base − actual ≈ 0` and every shutdown hour with the inequality constraint trivially satisfied. A least-squares fit over N samples extracts the underlying coefficient even when each individual sample carries tiny SNR. Algorithm: 3×3 normal equations + Cramer's rule (same machinery as cold-start), with a collinear 1D-projection fallback when the determinant is degenerate; result clamped to `[0, SOLAR_COEFF_CAP]`; damped against the existing coefficient at factor 0.3 (`new = 0.3 × batch + 0.7 × current`). Empty-prior regimes get the batch result directly — no signal to preserve. Conservative defaults ahead of field validation: `BATCH_FIT_MIN_SAMPLES = 30` (skip when noisy), `BATCH_FIT_DAMPING = 0.3` (revise after a season of data), `BATCH_FIT_SATURATION_RATIO = 0.95` (drop saturated samples that carry no information about the coefficient slope). Filter gates mirror live NLMS: shutdown samples excluded (inequality handles those), auxiliary-active hours excluded, below-`min_base` samples excluded, MPC-managed (`WeightedSmear`) sensors skipped (no coherent dark-equivalent baseline). Per (unit, regime) — heating and cooling are fit independently per the mode-stratification introduced earlier in this release. `diagnose_solar` per-unit output now carries a `last_batch_fit` block (timestamp, regime sample counts, residual RMSE, before/after coefficients, damping applied, skip reasons) so users can verify the fit's quality without re-running the service. The fourth coefficient signal path alongside NLMS (drift), inequality (shutdown-edge), and cold-start (bootstrap); ownership boundaries documented in `CLAUDE.md` Solar Model section. On-demand only via service call.
- **Per-entity screen scope (`screen_affected_entities`).** New config list — analog of `aux_affected_entities` — that narrows the installation-level `screen_config` to a subset of energy sensors. Entities not in the list effectively get `screen_config=(False, False, False)`: their solar coefficients learn and predict against pure transmittance=1.0 regardless of the global slider. Previously, a unit in a zone without external screens still absorbed an average transmittance it never physically experienced, biasing its per-direction coefficient. Default on upgrade: all energy sensors included (preserves prior behaviour). Uncheck a sensor in the config flow if its zone has no screens. Applies to live learning, live prediction (`calculate_total_power` per-entity loop, `_calculate_fallback_projection`), and retrain replay (`replay_solar_nlms`). Global state — potential battery, solar_factor, global `correlation_data` — keeps using the installation-level `screen_config` directly.
- **Mode-stratified per-unit solar coefficients.** Per-unit solar coefficients are now split by operating mode: `solar_coefficients_per_unit[entity_id]` carries `{"heating": {s, e, w}, "cooling": {s, e, w}}` instead of one shared dict. Heating-mode hours update only the heating regime; cooling-mode hours update only the cooling regime. Each absorbs its own `E[1/COP_mode]`: heating COP rises with outdoor temperature; cooling COP falls with outdoor temperature. The pre-1.3.4 unified coefficient was a weighted mean of the two physically distinct values, biasing whichever mode dominated the data — typically by 30-80 % in the cooling direction on heating-dominated installs. The cold-start buffer is also per-regime; the dead-zone counter is keyed per (entity, regime), so a stuck cooling coefficient resets without disturbing heating. Mode → regime mapping centralised in `learning._solar_coeff_regime`: HEATING + GUEST_HEATING → "heating"; COOLING + GUEST_COOLING → "cooling"; OFF / DHW → no learning. Inequality learning (1.3.3) writes the heating regime only — cooling shutdown semantics invert the constraint and remain out of scope. **Storage migration:** schema bumped to v4 (`_migrate_v3_to_v4`); existing flat coefficients are wrapped into `{"heating": old, "cooling": old}` so manually-seeded values survive the upgrade and live data drift naturally. **Read-side change:** `SolarCalculator.calculate_unit_coefficient(entity_id, temp_key, mode)` now requires `mode` as an argument; the heuristic that derived mode from `temp_key` is removed. Empty regime entries fall back to mode-appropriate defaults (`DEFAULT_SOLAR_COEFF_HEATING = 0.35`, `DEFAULT_SOLAR_COEFF_COOLING = 0.40`). **Diagnose_solar additions:** new per-unit fields `current_coefficient_heating`, `current_coefficient_cooling`, and a scalar `coefficient_split_delta_pct` (percentage divergence between regimes, averaged over directions). The pre-1.3.4 `current_coefficient` field is preserved as the heating regime so existing dashboards keep working.

### Changed
- **Mode-stratified per-unit base buckets.** Per-unit cooling-mode samples now route to a dedicated `"cooling"` wind-bucket at both the write boundary (`learning._process_per_unit_learning`) and the read boundaries (prediction, observation count, fallback projection). Heating samples continue to use `normal` / `high_wind` / `extreme_wind` as before. The two sample spaces coexist inside the existing `[entity][temp][wind_bucket]` structure with no schema change and no storage migration — `coordinator._get_wind_bucket()` never produces `"cooling"`, so heating and cooling cannot collide. Cooling prediction blocks the existing heating-wind fallback chain to prevent a sparse cooling bucket from silently returning heating data at the same temp (physically wrong at cooling temperatures): an empty cooling bucket returns 0 during the seasonal warm-up period rather than crossing over. Global `correlation_data` is unchanged — cooling continues to fold in via the existing sign-flipped `solar_normalization_delta`.
- **Removed cooling-at-cold guard (1.3.3).** The guard in `_process_per_unit_learning` that skipped cooling-mode updates when `T < balance_point - 2` existed specifically to prevent cooling-idle standby from contaminating heating-dominated per-unit buckets. With cooling samples now landing in a dedicated bucket, cold-hour cooling standby populates `cooling[cold_temp]["cooling"]` — semantically correct (those are cooling observations) and cannot leak into heating. The guard and its targeted test file are removed.

## [1.3.3] - 2026-04-24

### Added
- **Per-unit solar-learning noise floor with auto-calibration.** NLMS solar coefficient learning, the parallel inequality learner, and the solar-shutdown detector are now gated on a per-sensor `min_base` threshold derived from each unit's dark-hour (`solar_factor < 0.05`) actual consumption. The threshold is each unit's p10 of dark-hour samples over the last 30 days, floored at 0.03 kWh, with a ±50 % rate-of-change limit per recalibration. Two guards reject distributions that cannot represent a modulating noise floor: a ratio-guard rejects when `p10 / median > 0.9` (sensor is measuring an always-on load — electric boiler mislabeled as heat-pump heating, sensor scoped to a shared circuit) and an absolute ceiling at 1.5 kWh acts as a safety net sized for residential heat pumps up to ~10–12 kW nameplate running continuously at minimum modulation. Fresh installs and units with fewer than 20 dark-hour samples fall back to a global 0.15 kWh default. Calibration runs opportunistically at every startup once 14 days of log data exist, and on-demand via the new `calibrate_unit_thresholds` service. Motivation: the 0.15 kWh default was grounded in one reference install's heat pump minimum-modulation floor, and left several classes of real units permanently outside the learning loop — low-min-modulation heat pumps (Panasonic 3 kW, Toshiba at mild temperatures), larger heat pumps with 500–1000 W minimum modulation, and small electric loads (thermostats, underfloor cables, socket circuits). Per-unit thresholds let each sensor's coefficient learn against its own noise floor. `diagnose_solar.per_unit_thresholds` surfaces the effective value per unit and whether it was auto-calibrated or using the fallback. Thresholds survive `reset_solar_learning` — they are hardware noise floors, not learned state.
- **Cooling-at-cold guard in per-unit base EMA.** Units in `MODE_COOLING` with outdoor temperature below `balance_point − 2` are now excluded from per-unit base-EMA updates. Rationale: the seasonal-automation pattern — where a user sets cooling mode for a whole summer and the unit idles on cold nights — produces low-magnitude standby consumption that contaminated the heating-dominated per-unit buckets. One-line guard in `LearningManager._process_per_unit_learning`; threshold matches the transition-zone boundary in `diagnose_solar.temperature_stratified`. Global base learning is intentionally unaffected (the standby contamination there is <1 % seasonal bias). Retrain paths (`replay_per_unit_models`, `learn_from_historical_import`) are NOT guarded — they replay the log faithfully so historical data is preserved for future mode-stratified work. Known limitations: commercial / data-center cooling at cold temperatures (real cooling demand discarded), high-balance-point installations (shoulder-season cooling data may be lost), and mode-misconfiguration scenarios are not fully addressed; the proper fix is mode-stratified per-unit base buckets.
- **Inequality learning for solar shutdown hours.** Heat pump units whose thermostat cuts the compressor during sun (detected by `detect_solar_shutdown_entities`) previously had their shutdown hours discarded as learning signal — producing a permanent ceiling on per-direction coefficient convergence for low-demand units (the Toshiba `coeff_w` Catch-22 observed in the field). A parallel learner now runs on these hours with inequality semantics: `coeff · battery_filtered_potential ≥ INEQUALITY_MARGIN × base` (margin default 0.9). When the constraint is violated, a projected-gradient step with size `INEQUALITY_STEP_SIZE = 0.05` (half of NLMS) raises the coefficients proportional to the non-zero components of the battery-filtered potential vector. The learner is **strictly one-sided** — it can only lift coefficients; NLMS on modulating-regime hours handles the downward correction. Signal is battery-filtered (not instantaneous): shutdown is an accumulated thermal-mass phenomenon, and the learner's input uses three new `_potential_battery_s/e/w` per-direction EMA states that share the existing `solar_battery_decay` (0.80 default, ~3.1h half-life). Cooling mode is out of scope — the inversion semantics would require a symmetric constraint not yet validated. Non-negative and `SOLAR_COEFF_CAP` clamps are enforced consistently with the NLMS path.
- **`diagnose_solar.implied_coefficient_inequality` per-unit section.** Shadow replay of the inequality learner + NLMS over the same window as other implied-coefficient diagnostics, starting from zero. Reported alongside `implied_coefficient_30d_no_shutdown` — the delta between the two shows how much lift the inequality path would add. Aggregate counters in the top-level `inequality_replay` block report `updates`, `non_binding` (constraint satisfied), `skipped_low_battery` (battery not yet populated), `skipped_mode` (cooling), `skipped_base` (below `SOLAR_SHUTDOWN_MIN_BASE`). `enabled` reflects the current flag state so analysis tooling can interpret zero-counters correctly.
- **Persistent per-direction potential batteries in storage.** Three new fields `potential_battery_s/e/w` persisted alongside the existing `solar_battery_state`. Default 0.0 on missing — legacy installations repopulate the EMA within a few sunny hours post-upgrade. No migration required.
- **SNR-weighted base-model learning.** Replaces the `solar_normalization_delta` compensation in base-bucket learning with a signal-to-noise weight derived from the hour's observed conditions. Target becomes raw `actual_kwh`; the EMA step size is scaled by `snr_weight(solar_factor, solar_dominant_entities, total_units)` — dark hours retain full rate, sunny hours contribute proportionally (linearly decaying from 1.0 to `SNR_WEIGHT_FLOOR = 0.1` as `solar_factor` crosses `1 / SNR_WEIGHT_K` toward clear-sky), hours where all units are in shutdown contribute zero, and partially-shutdown hours scale by the clean fraction. Applies to the global base EMA (`process_learning`), per-unit base EMA (composed with the existing headroom multiplier via multiplication), Track A hourly retrain (per-entry weight), and Track B daily retrain (day-average `solar_factor` drives a day-level weight; delta dropped from the daily target to preserve dark-equivalent bucket semantics via the weighted mean). Validated on user data: analytical steady-state RMSE vs dark-hour ground truth drops 61.8 % on 90-day window, 67.2 % on 30-day window; zero cold-bucket degradation across the weight-function sweep. Weight-function parameters (`SNR_WEIGHT_FLOOR`, `SNR_WEIGHT_K`) live in `const.py` and may be exposed via calibration service in a future release. **Expected transient:** existing installations' base buckets gradually converge toward dark-hour empirical means over ~60 days of continued live learning. Users who want immediate convergence can run `retrain_from_history` after the upgrade. Bucket values may shift by up to 10–20 % on mild-weather buckets previously inflated by solar contamination; shifts are in the correcting direction and align with what `base_model_health` has been flagging as `inflated`.
- **Retrain simplifies to 1 pass + orthogonal NLMS replay.** The 3-pass EM-lite orchestration that prior versions used to break the base↔solar circularity from delta-based normalization is no longer required: SNR weighting ignores the delta during base learning, so the circularity vanishes. Both Track A (hourly) and Track B (daily) retrain paths simplify accordingly. Response field `em_passes` reports `1`. NLMS replay itself is unchanged.
- **`diagnose_model` base-model health via dark-hour replay.** New `base_model_health` section in `diagnose_model` compares each stored `(temp_key, wind_bucket)` value against the empirical mean `actual_kwh` over dark (solar_factor < 0.05) non-aux all-heating hours from the hourly log. Dark hours have no solar signal to normalise, so their mean is an independent ground-truth for what the bucket should have converged to — uncontaminated by any bias the solar learning loop may have introduced during winter. Reports per-bucket `{stored_kwh, actual_dark_mean_kwh, delta_kwh, delta_pct, verdict}` and groups buckets into `inflated`, `deflated`, `unverifiable` flag lists (sorted worst-first). Thresholds: `min_dark_hours_for_verdict = 10`, `bucket_deviation_threshold_pct = 15`. Existing monotonicity check only catches local inversions; `base_model_health` catches the harder case of a whole region (e.g. mild-weather buckets) being uniformly inflated by winter solar contamination while remaining monotonic. Automates the manual recalibration pattern: given observed "cloudy mild day over-predicting by 20 %", this section surfaces the offending bucket(s) with quantified deviation.
- **`diagnose_solar` temperature-regime stratification.** New per-unit `temperature_stratified` section splits the modeled-vs-implied delta into three buckets aligned to the configured `balance_point`: `heating_deep` (T < BP − 8, low COP / defrost / aux-regime), `heating_mild` (BP − 8 ≤ T < BP − 2, optimal heat-pump range), and `cooling` (T > BP + 2, inverted solar semantics). The ±2 °C transition zone around balance_point is dropped because mode flips hour-to-hour there and the signal would be pure noise. Reporting-only in this pass (no threshold flags) — quantitative thresholds will be set once European summer data reveals what "significant COP-correlated bias" looks like empirically. This is the first instrument capable of falsifying the documented coefficient COP-blindness: if `heating_deep.mean_delta` differs systematically from `heating_mild.mean_delta`, the coefficient is absorbing COP variation; if `cooling.mean_delta` differs from `heating_mild.mean_delta`, the mode-inverted solar-normalization path is learning separately from the heating path.
- **`diagnose_solar` transmittance validation sections.** Three additions surface whether the 1.3.3 screen-transmittance values (`SCREEN_DIRECT_TRANSMITTANCE = 0.08`, `DEFAULT_SOLAR_MIN_TRANSMITTANCE = 0.30`) match the installation's actual physics. (1) **`context`** top-level block reports latitude, longitude, `screen_config`, and the constants in effect — so a diagnose payload is self-identifying for remote analysis across geographically diverse installations. (2) **`screen_stratified`** per-unit section splits the modeled-vs-implied delta into `open` (correction ≥ 80 %), `mid` (40–80 %), and `closed` (< 40 %) buckets; when both extremes have ≥ 10 hours the `bias_gap_kwh = closed_mean − open_mean` surfaces directly. A gap > 0.05 kWh flags `transmittance_floor_too_high`; < −0.05 flags `transmittance_floor_too_low`. (3) **`transmittance_sensitivity`** per-unit sweep re-solves the 3×3 normal equations with candidate floor values {0.05, 0.08, 0.12, 0.15, 0.20, 0.25} reconstructed from stored effective vectors; reports residual RMSE per candidate and the empirically optimal value. Gated on ≥ 20 qualifying hours and ≥ 40 percentage points of correction_percent variance (`informative` flag). When the optimum deviates > 0.04 from 0.08 in an informative sweep, `sensitivity_suggests_transmittance_retune` is flagged. Enables validating the literature-derived constants against real European screen-deployment data from mid-April onward without waiting for the Norwegian dev's own solar-poor environment to produce signal.
- **Per-direction screen transmittance with user configuration.** Three new config booleans — `screen_south`, `screen_east`, `screen_west` — let users declare which facades have external motorised screens. Each cardinal direction's transmittance is now computed independently: a configured-screened facade ramps from `SCREEN_DIRECT_TRANSMITTANCE` (0.08, screen fabric × triple glass) at slider 0 % up to 1.0 at slider 100 %; an unscreened facade is fixed at 1.0 regardless of the slider. Closing south-facade screens therefore no longer pulls down the model's east-window estimate when only south is screened. The pre-1.3.3 single-floor model effectively coupled all three directions through one global transmittance — physically wrong for typical Nordic homes where 10–25 % of windows (north walls, utility rooms, skylights) are unscreened. Defaults: all three booleans True on upgrade so the composite behaviour matches a raised global floor (see *Changed*). Canonical implementation: `SolarCalculator._screen_transmittance_vector(correction_percent, screen_config)`.
- **Solar shutdown regime detection for VP units.** Heat pump units whose thermostat cuts the compressor during sun — a scenario where the thermostat reaches setpoint while external screens are open — previously drove two destructive learning signals every sunny hour: an inflated `actual_impact = base − 0 = base` for the solar coefficient, and a contaminated `solar_normalization_delta` for the base model. New `detect_solar_shutdown_entities` (in `observation.py`) flags such hours using only this-hour data (no historical tracking, no circular dependencies on the learned coefficient): magnitude of the potential (pre-screen) solar vector above `SOLAR_SHUTDOWN_MIN_MAGNITUDE` (0.3), expected base above `SOLAR_SHUTDOWN_MIN_BASE` (0.15 kWh), and either actual below `SOLAR_SHUTDOWN_ACTUAL_FLOOR` (0.03 kWh) or actual/base ratio below `SOLAR_SHUTDOWN_RATIO` (0.15). Flags are stored on `HourlyObservation` and in the hourly log (`solar_regime`, `solar_dominant_entities`).
- **`diagnose_solar` shutdown section.** Per-unit reporting now includes `shutdown_hours_30d`, `shutdown_pct_of_qualifying`, and a parallel `implied_coefficient_30d_no_shutdown` solve that excludes shutdown hours — letting users see how much shutdown bias inflated the learned coefficient. A new `solar_shutdown_detected` flag appears when ≥5 shutdown hours are observed in the 30-day window.
- **`bp_at_log_time` field in hourly log entries.** Each hourly log entry now records the `balance_point` that was live when the entry was written. The stored `tdd` value is a function of `abs(BP − avg_temp)`, so changing BP via the reconfigure flow leaves historical `tdd` frozen under the old BP while new entries use the new BP. Without this field, retrospective analyses (`diagnose_model`, `diagnose_solar`, retrain) that sum or stratify across the log are silently mixing values computed under different thresholds. This is observability-only — consumers do not yet read the field; it is a prerequisite for future BP-aware diagnostics. Legacy entries (pre-1.3.3) lack the field and must be treated as unknown-BP by any consumer that eventually uses it.
- **`days_skipped_mpc_unavailable` field in `retrain_from_history` response.** Retrain responses now expose the count of days skipped because Track C was enabled but `track_c_distribution` was missing. Applies to both MPC outages and pre-Track-C historical days — see *Changed* below. Always `0` on non-Track-C installs.
- **`track_c_outage_session_count` field in `diagnose_model`.** Runtime counter of live-midnight-sync days where Track C was enabled but MPC returned no distribution. Resets on HA restart. Surfaces whether an install is seeing frequent MPC unavailability.
- **Track C pre-midnight MPC snapshot.** The midnight sync runs at 00:xx of the new day and processes yesterday's data. If MPC happens to be unavailable at exactly that moment (HA startup race, service loading, busy kernel), previously the entire day was lost. The coordinator now pre-fetches the MPC buffer at 22:00, 23:00, and 23:55 the evening before; the midnight sync falls back to the most recent snapshot when the live call fails. Loses at most a few minutes of data from the end of the day (absorbed by EMA). The outage skip is reserved for the pathological case where HA was down from before 22:00 through after 00:01. Transient state — not persisted across restarts.
- **`track_used` field in `daily_history` entries.** Each daily_history entry now records which track produced its values: `"A"` (per-hour meter, non-daily-learning), `"B_flat"` (daily q/24 fallback), `"B_cop"` (COP-smeared experimental path), `"C_live"` (Track C live MPC success), `"C_snapshot_2200"`/`"C_snapshot_2300"`/`"C_snapshot_2355"` (Track C using pre-midnight snapshot), `"skipped_mpc_outage"` (outage skip). Legacy entries (pre-1.3.3) lack the field — consumers should treat absence as `"unknown"`. Foundation for future track-composition diagnostics; `diagnose_model` does not yet read it.

### Changed
- **Composite legacy floor raised 0.20 → 0.30.** `DEFAULT_SOLAR_MIN_TRANSMITTANCE` now applies only when `screen_config` is absent (legacy/pre-1.3.3 storage). The 0.20 value fit only fully-screened bespoke installations; 0.30 better represents a typical Nordic home where 10–25 % of window area is unscreened. Existing installations on upgrade get the legacy fallback via the per-direction default of `(True, True, True)`, which behaves like a single composite floor with the new 0.30 value plus per-direction structural correctness. **Expected transient:** solar coefficients re-converge against the new transmittance distribution as screens deploy this season — typically within 10 qualifying sunny hours per direction (days to ~2 weeks in Nordic spring). Predictions during the transient may show small biases that self-correct via NLMS. No user action required; users who want immediate convergence can run `reset_solar_learning`.
- **Per-unit base EMA rate weighted by solar headroom.** `unit_learning_rate = min(learning_rate, PER_UNIT_LEARNING_RATE_CAP) × headroom`, where `headroom = max(0, base − unit_solar_impact) / base`. At full saturation the EMA freezes; at dark hours the multiplier is 1.0 (unchanged behaviour). This breaks the second link of the solar-shutdown feedback loop without touching the formula — invariant #1 (normalization ≡ prediction) is preserved because only the step size changes, not the target. Applied only in the normal (non-aux) branch; aux-fallback keeps the existing rate.
- **Solar NLMS skipped for shutdown-flagged entities.** `_learn_unit_solar_coefficient` is bypassed for entities in `solar_dominant_entities`. Other entities in the same hour continue to learn normally. The dead-zone counter is untouched so existing recovery logic still fires when a true dead zone develops.
- **Solar battery decay default raised from 0.75 to 0.80.** Half-life increases from ~2.4h to ~3.1h, better matching typical Norwegian construction with concrete floor slabs. Post-sunset carry-over lasts ~30% longer. Users who have calibrated via `diagnose_solar` with `apply_battery_decay: true` are unaffected (their value is in `entry.data`).
- **`solar_normalization_delta` applied only to DirectMeter contributions.** The delta was designed to normalize raw-electrical values to a dark-sky equivalent for Track A base-model learning. `apply_strategies_to_global_model` previously applied it to the combined hourly sum of all strategy contributions, which silently double-counted when `WeightedSmear` (Track C's MPC-synthetic thermal → per-hour COP path) was also present: the synthetic values already embed solar attenuation via the smearing weights. On pure Track C installs the delta is structurally 0 (no non-MPC units have NLMS coefficients, so heating_solar_applied - cooling_solar_applied sums to 0) and the change is a no-op. On mixed installs (MPC heat pump + unmetered or weakly-metered resistive backup, like panel heaters on a separate circuit), the delta now applies to the DirectMeter sum only — preventing the smear portion from being inflated by non-MPC units' solar correction. The delta is also suppressed entirely on hours where no DirectMeter actually contributed, so a legacy-nonzero delta cannot leak into a smear-only bucket. No migration required; the correction is forward-only and bucket EMA self-calibrates over ~60 days.
- **Track C installs: skip learning on days without `track_c_distribution`.** Track C and Track B produce different kinds of `q_adjusted` — Track C is MPC-synthetic thermal-per-hour, Track B is raw electrical (optionally thermal-mass-corrected). Writing both kinds of values into the same `correlation_data` and U-coefficient EMA is a category error, not a precision problem. Applies in two places: (1) live midnight sync — when `track_c_enabled=True` and MPC returns no distribution, the bucket update and U-coefficient update are both skipped (previously a Track B thermal-mass-corrected fallback was applied); (2) retrain — a Track C install's retrain now skips any day in `daily_history` that lacks `track_c_distribution`. The retrain skip covers both MPC outages AND pre-Track-C history (days logged before Track C was enabled on this install). Users who want pre-Track-C days included in retrain must either narrow `days_back` to the post-Track-C window or temporarily disable Track C. Non-Track-C installs are unaffected. The `experimental_cop_smear` flag is out of scope for this fix and remains available on non-Track-C installs.

## [1.3.2] - 2026-04-17

### Added
- **3-component solar vector decomposition (S, E, W) (#832).** The solar model now decomposes sunlight into three cardinal direction components — south, east, and west — using non-negative basis functions (`max(0, -cos(az))`, `max(0, sin(az))`, `max(0, -sin(az))`). Previously, the 2D decomposition used a signed east component (`sin(az)`, negative in the afternoon), making it impossible to model west-facing windows because the non-negative coefficient clamp prevented learning a negative east coefficient. The 3-component system: (1) captures west-facing window solar gain, which was previously unmodeled (~23% of solar gain lost for typical buildings); (2) eliminates a high-latitude summer learning bias where the south component went negative when the sun was north of due east/west; (3) makes the `>= 0` coefficient clamp a true physics invariant (each window direction can only receive gain, never produce negative gain). East and west components are orthogonal by construction (disjoint temporal support — they never overlap). NLMS convergence, cold-start 2×2→3×3 least squares, and `diagnose_solar` normal equations are all updated. Existing coefficients migrate automatically (`"w"` defaults to 0.0); the west coefficient converges organically via NLMS within 3-7 sunny days. Base model buckets self-correct via EMA over 60-90 days; `retrain_from_history` can accelerate this.
- **Configurable hourly log retention (#820).** The hourly log retention is now user-configurable via the Advanced Options page in the config flow: 90 days (default, conservative for Raspberry Pi), 180 days, or 365 days. Longer retention preserves cross-seasonal data for `retrain_from_history`, `diagnose_solar`, and `diagnose_model`. Reducing the value permanently trims hourly data beyond the new limit; daily summaries are not affected. Existing installations default to 90 days (backwards-compatible, no migration needed).
- **Daily history retention limit removed (#820).** Daily history (~200 bytes/day) no longer has a 730-day cap and grows indefinitely. Ten years of data is under 730 KB. This gives `compare_periods` unlimited historical range.

### Changed
- **Solar battery model changed from leaky integrator to EMA.** The previous formula `state = state × decay + solar_impact` amplified the solar signal by `1/(1−decay)` — 4× at decay 0.75, 6.7× at decay 0.85. This violated energy conservation: the battery credited more heat release than was ever absorbed, creating phantom heating demand at night. The new EMA formula `state = state × decay + solar_impact × (1 − decay)` is the exact discretisation of Newton's law of cooling. At steady state, effective solar equals raw solar input (no amplification). The decay parameter now purely controls the time constant. Existing battery states are automatically migrated on first load. The `diagnose_solar` calibration sweep uses the same EMA formula.

- **Forecast now uses 3D solar vectors for per-unit impact.** The forecast path previously used scalar `calculate_solar_factor` and reconstructed a vector using the building's static azimuth, attributing all solar to the south direction regardless of actual sun position. This meant learned east/west coefficients were ignored in forecasts — a pre-existing limitation that became material with the 3D decomposition. The hourly forecast (`_process_forecast_item`), daily forecast (`_calculate_from_daily_forecast`), reference map (`_build_reference_map`), and deviation analysis all now compute and pass `override_solar_vector` to `calculate_total_power`. The scalar `calculate_solar_factor` is retained for the solar optimizer (which uses it for state classification, not directional impact). New `estimate_daily_avg_solar_vector` method in `solar.py` for the daily forecast path.

### Fixed
- **Fallback projection had zero solar correction.** The `_calculate_fallback_projection` path (used for per-unit prediction during the first 1-2 minutes after HA restart) read `solar_vector_s/e/w` from `coordinator.data`, but the minute-level update loop never wrote them there. All three keys returned the `.get()` default of 0.0, so the fallback prediction had no solar correction — slightly over-predicting on sunny hours during the brief post-restart window.
- **`diagnose_model` crashed with NameError when `days_back=0`.** The `cutoff_str` variable was only defined inside a conditional branch but referenced unconditionally in three places (mode contamination, solar correlation, Track B filtering). Fixed by initialising unconditionally.
- **`diagnose_model` solar correlation interpretation was inverted.** Positive correlation (actual > expected at high solar) was labelled "over-subtracting" but means the opposite: the solar model subtracts too little. Swapped the captions.
- **Track C and COP-smear battery pre-pass used old leaky integrator (#830).** Both midnight battery simulation loops still used `state × decay + raw_solar` instead of the EMA formula. With decay 0.75 this over-weighted solar residual by up to 4× in post-sunset loss weights, affecting all Track C users and `retrain_from_history` with `experimental_cop_smear`.
- **`HourlyObservation.correction_percent` defaulted to 0% (screens closed).** The legacy learning path never set this field, so transmittance was 0.20 instead of 1.0, inflating the potential vector by 5×. Default changed to 100% (screens open).
- **Hourly log retention severed cached model reference.** The retention policy `self._hourly_log = self._hourly_log[-2160:]` created a new list object, but the cached `ModelState` (via the `model` property) held a reference to the old list. After the trim — triggered when the log first reaches 2160 entries, typically on the first hour boundary after a restart with 90 days of history — `model.hourly_log[-1]` was permanently stuck at the entry that caused the trim. Sensor attributes (`last_hour_timestamp`, `model_updated_temp_category`, `model_value_before/after`) showed stale data from hours ago while the sensor state (deviation kWh/%) updated correctly. Fixed by using in-place deletion (`del self._hourly_log[:-2160]`), preserving list object identity.
- **Solar coefficient dead zone detection.** When the per-unit base model is too low (expected < actual during sun), the `max(0, expected - actual)` clamp produces zero for every sunny hour. NLMS receives no signal and the coefficient is trapped. After 15 consecutive qualifying hours with zero impact, the coefficient is reset to cold-start. Additionally, cold-start buffers filled entirely with zero-impact samples are discarded instead of solved — preventing a re-entry loop where the reset produces a (0,0,0) coefficient that immediately re-enters the dead zone. The unit stays in cold-start (using the default coefficient 0.35 for normalization) until the base model recovers enough to produce positive actual_impact.

## [1.3.1] - 2026-04-08

### Added
- **`diagnose_solar` service.** New diagnostic service for per-unit solar coefficient validation and global solar model health. Reports implied coefficients (back-calculated via normal equations), coefficient stability across 3 time windows, saturation frequency, temporal bias (morning vs afternoon), battery decay health (post-sunset residual analysis), screen correction impact (stratified error comparison), and hour-of-day residual curve. Includes battery decay calibration: sweeps decay rates 0.50–0.95 and recommends the optimal value. Set `apply_battery_decay: true` to persist the recommendation. Single-pass over hourly log, same pattern as `diagnose_model`.

### Changed
- **Solar coefficient learning switched from LMS to NLMS.** The standard LMS gradient descent had gain-dependent instability: high-solar units (e.g. large south-facing windows) received updates proportional to solar vector magnitude squared, causing oscillation. NLMS (Normalized LMS) divides the step by input power, making convergence rate independent of solar magnitude. The epsilon regularization parameter (0.05) naturally shrinks the east coefficient when the east signal is weak (typical at 60°N). Replaces the `PER_UNIT_LEARNING_RATE_CAP` for solar learning (kept for base/aux).
- **Solar coefficients learned against potential (pre-screen) solar vector.** Previously, per-unit solar coefficients were learned against the effective (post-screen) vector, coupling screen state into the coefficient. Screen automation caused coefficient drift — windowless rooms accumulated phantom coefficients as the global correction oscillated. Coefficients now learn against the potential (pre-screen) vector and implicitly absorb the average screen transmittance: `impact = coefficient × potential_vector`. Both the prediction and normalization paths use the same formula — no separate screen transmittance factor is applied. Existing coefficients will naturally re-converge within 1-2 weeks of sunny hours.
- **Cloud coverage exponent corrected from 1.5 to 3.4 (Kasten & Czeplak 1980).** The previous exponent was introduced in v1.3.0 as an approximation of the Kasten formula but was incorrectly transcribed — the published value is 3.4. The difference is significant at partial cloud coverage: at 50% cloud the old exponent reduced solar by 27% while Kasten gives 7%. The error was not constant across cloud levels (2% at clear sky, 39% at 70% cloud), forcing the per-unit coefficient to absorb a cloud-level-dependent bias instead of representing stable window physics. With exponent 3.4, the bias is nearly constant (~1%) across all cloud levels, making the coefficient portable across weather providers and stable across seasons. Existing coefficients are inflated by the absorbed cloud bias (~1.2–1.4×) and will re-converge via NLMS within 10–20 qualifying solar hours.
- **Solar battery decay default raised from 0.60 to 0.75.** The previous half-life of 1.4 hours only captured light thermal mass (furniture, interior surfaces). The new half-life of ~2.4 hours is a middle ground that works for both lightweight (furniture, interior surfaces) and heavier (concrete, floor heating) construction without over-crediting residual solar in the evening hours. Per-installation calibration is available via `diagnose_solar` service with `apply_battery_decay: true`. The decay rate is stored in `entry.data` and can be overridden without config flow changes.
- **Per-unit mode selectors always created.** Mode select entities (heating/cooling/off/dhw/guest) are now created for every energy sensor regardless of the `has_ac_units` toggle. Previously, users without AC could not set DHW or Guest modes — critical for correct mode filtering of hot water energy and visitor impact. The `has_ac_units` toggle has been removed from the config flow. Existing configurations are unaffected; the toggle value in `entry.data` is simply ignored.
- **Physics config step simplified.** Removed `has_ac_units` toggle and cleaned up stale field references (wind sensors and indoor temp sensor labels that had already moved to the feature config page).

### Fixed
- **`_validate_physics` dead wind sensor check removed.** The physics step validated `wind_speed_sensor` presence to skip weather entity wind checks, but the field was moved to the feature_config page in v1.3.0. The conditional was always false, making the validation effectively correct (always checks weather entity) but misleading. Now unconditionally validates that the weather entity supplies `wind_speed`.

## [1.3.0] - 2026-04-07

### Added
- **Thermodynamic Baseline Engine (Track C).** New optional learning mode that decouples the model from MPC load-shifting. At midnight, Heating Analytics fetches 24 hours of delivered thermal energy from a connected `heatpump_mpc` integration and redistributes it across hours proportionally to weather-driven heat loss (ΔT × wind × solar⁻¹). The synthetic baseline is converted to electrical via per-hour COP (see below), retaining Track A's hourly bucket resolution while eliminating the feedback loop where the model learns MPC scheduling decisions as building physics. COP is capped at 10.0 to prevent physically unrealistic values when outdoor temperature approaches LWT. ## [1.2.9] - 2026-03-27

### Added
- **Solar thermal battery.** Introduces an exponential decay accumulator (`_solar_battery_state`) that charges on each hour's raw solar impact and decays at a fixed rate (`SOLAR_BATTERY_DECAY = 0.60` per hour, ~20 % remaining after 3 hours). This models heat absorbed by the building mass during peak sun being released gradually over the following hours, rather than treating solar gain as an instantaneous per-hour subtraction. The smoothed `effective_solar_impact` replaces the raw value in the learning normalisation call (`normalize_for_learning`), the thermodynamic gross calculation, and the hourly log entry (`solar_impact_kwh`). ### Changed
- **`compare_periods` now tracks the data basis of each period explicitly.** Each period in the result carries a `basis` field (`"actual"` / `"modeled"` / `"hybrid"`) describing what the energy figures actually represent: `"actual"` means all days are past with real consumption measurements, `"modeled"` means no consumption was recorded (system not tracking that year), and `"hybrid"` means the period spans today or future (mix of actuals and forecast). ## [1.2.6] - 2026-03-08

### Added
- **DHW mode support for air-to-water heat pumps.** Units that periodically switch to domestic hot water production now have a dedicated `MODE_DHW` state. During a DHW cycle the heat pump delivers zero heat to the space; the model correctly observes this as zero contribution and learns accordingly. The previous behaviour treated DHW like `MODE_OFF` (skipping the learning update entirely), which caused systematic upward drift in the learned coefficient proportional to the DHW duty cycle — equivalent to a heating-cable hysteresis error. Setting a unit to `MODE_DHW` eliminates the drift at all learning rates. At the default learning rate (1%) the steady-state oscillation amplitude stays within ±7% of the true coefficient even in extreme DHW-heavy operating profiles.
- **Blueprints for automatic mode state mapping** are now included in the repository under `blueprints/`. `heat_pump_mode_sync.yaml` maps a heat pump's operation mode sensor (Heating / Domestic Hot Water / Defrost) to the Heating Analytics mode select helper automatically — Defrost is handled transparently by preserving the current mode so defrost energy is attributed to the active heating or DHW cycle. `climate_sync.yaml` maps standard climate entity states (heat / cool / off) to the mode helper, with an optional guest mode prefix for tracking occupancy separately.
- **Solar screen transmittance floor (`MIN_SOLAR_TRANSMITTANCE = 0.20`).** The previous screen correction applied a pure linear scale (`effective = correction_percent / 100`), collapsing the effective solar vector to zero when all screens were closed. This suppressed solar-coefficient learning (the `vector_magnitude ≤ 0.01` guard in the learning engine returns early when the vector is near-zero) and forced the base thermal model to silently absorb residual solar gain as a systematic downward bias in temperature-bucket coefficients. Physically, a closed screen still transmits ~10–15 % of solar irradiance through its fabric (G-value/SHGC), and unmonitored windows without screens contribute additional baseline gain. The new formula `effective = MIN + (1 − MIN) × (correction_percent / 100)` ensures the effective vector stays non-zero at all screen positions. At 0 % open: effective = 0.20; at 100 % open: effective = 1.0 (unchanged). Existing learned solar coefficients were calibrated against the old scale and will converge to corrected values via EMA (lr = 0.01) over a few weeks of sunny weather — no manual reset required.

### Fixed
- **Multiple instances share the same storage file.** The storage key was the fixed string `heating_analytics.storage`, causing all instances on the same HA installation to read and write the same file — each restart overwrote the other's learned model with its own data. Each `StorageManager` instance now uses a per-entry key (`heating_analytics.storage_<entry_id>`). On first load with the new code, a one-time migration from the legacy shared file is attempted only if the stored `daily_history` contains at least one sensor that is also configured for the instance — a mismatch means the stored data belongs to a different instance, so that instance starts fresh instead. Existing single-instance installations migrate their learned data automatically; new or secondary instances start with a clean slate.

## [1.2.4] - 2026-03-06

### Added
- Added `test_asymmetric` flag to `calibrate_inertia` service. When enabled, evaluates a regime-based asymmetric inertia model alongside the standard symmetric scan: slow profile (4 h) when outdoor temperature is falling (heat shedding), fast profile (2 h) when rising (heat gaining), and a stable 3 h profile otherwise. Returns comparative R², RMSE, delta_r2 vs best symmetric, and a regime breakdown (shedding/gaining/stable hour counts).
- Added `test_delta_t_scaling` flag to `calibrate_inertia` service. Bins pure hours by (balance_point − outdoor_temp) in 5 °C steps and finds the optimal exponential τ per bin (`window = min(5τ, 168)`), consistent with the coordinator's runtime model. Tests the hypothesis that thermal inertia scales with temperature differential: low ΔT (mild weather) → short τ, high ΔT (deep winter) → long τ. A monotonic `best_tau` trend across bins confirms the hypothesis and would support a future ΔT-adaptive inertia model.

### Fixed
- **Solar learning buffer is no longer partitioned by temperature bucket.** The per-unit solar coefficient (`c_s`, `c_e`) models window area, orientation, and shading — physical properties that do not depend on outdoor temperature. Partitioning the cold-start buffer by `temp_key` caused it to accumulate at most 1–2 samples per bucket before the temperature drifted to the next bucket, so `LEARNING_BUFFER_THRESHOLD = 4` was never reached in any single bucket and jump-start never fired. The buffer is now a single flat list per unit (pooling solar observations across all temperatures), and the learned coefficient is stored globally per unit rather than per `(unit, temp_key)`. The `calculate_unit_coefficient` lookup in `solar.py` is simplified accordingly. On first load, storage migration flattens existing temp-stratified buffers into the new format and picks the first valid entry from any existing temp-stratified coefficient store.
- **Thermal inertia kernel corrected from Gaussian to causal exponential decay.** The Gaussian kernel `exp(−½((t − μ)/σ)²)` places its peak weight at the midpoint of the lookback window (e.g. 2 hours ago for a 4-hour setting), not at the current observation. A building's thermal mass follows first-order (RC-circuit) dynamics: the indoor heat content decays toward the outdoor temperature at a rate governed by the time constant τ = C_a / UA, where C_a is the effective thermal capacitance and UA is the total heat loss coefficient. The correct weighting function is therefore a causal exponential decay `e^(−t/τ)`, which assigns maximum weight to the present state and monotonically less to older samples — physically equivalent to the impulse response of an RC low-pass filter. The Gaussian has no analogue in building thermodynamics and systematically biased the effective temperature toward historical observations, effectively overstating inertia and producing recommendations that were too conservative. Replaced with `generate_exponential_kernel(tau)` throughout.
- **Kernel window capped at 5τ hours (captures 99.3 % of cumulative weight).** Previously the exponential kernel used the full 168-hour default window regardless of τ. For τ = 4 h this meant `_get_inertia_parameters` requested 167 hours of history and weighted 99 % of that data at negligible values. Window is now `min(5τ, 168)`, consistent with the standard RC-circuit approximation where five time constants represent effective convergence to steady state.
- **Stale-data gap detection decoupled from kernel window size.** `_get_inertia_parameters` derived `max_gap` from `len(inertia_weights)`, which after the window cap became 5τ instead of τ. This disabled the thermal-discontinuity guard: after a system restart or sensor outage, temperature logs from up to 20 hours ago (for τ = 4) were treated as continuous rather than discarded. `max_gap` is now `int(tau)`, preserving the original behaviour that only consecutive hourly data within one time constant is considered thermodynamically continuous.
- **`calibrate_inertia` now recommends τ directly applicable to the config-flow slider.** The service previously returned `best_overall.hours` from a Gaussian sweep. Because the coordinator uses an exponential kernel, the Gaussian recommendation was not directly comparable: the Gaussian peak at h = 6 compensated for its non-causal weighting by artificially widening the window, while the physical time constant is τ = 4 h. The primary output is now `recommended_tau` from an exponential sweep (τ = 1–24 h, `window = min(5τ, 168)`), matching the coordinator's runtime model exactly. The Gaussian sweep is retained as `gaussian_best_hours` / `gaussian_best_r2` for comparison. Weekly stability analysis and the optional extended sweep (`test_exponential_kernel`, τ = 1–72 h) are also converted to exponential.
- **Inertia weights attribute now shows normalised values (sum = 1.0).** The `weights` field on the Thermal State sensor previously exposed raw kernel fractions normalised over the full window (e.g. sum ≈ 0.64 when 10 of 45 kernel positions were active). The internal calculation already re-normalises within the active subset, but the displayed values did not reflect this, making manual verification (`Σ history[i] × weight[i] ≈ effective_temperature`) impossible without dividing by the displayed sum. Weights are now re-normalised to the active sample count before display.

### Changed
- **Cold-start solar coefficient estimates are now dampened by 50% before publishing.** The 4-sample least squares estimate used for jump-start is computed while the base model is itself in early learning, making `actual_impact` noisy. This systematically inflates the initial coefficient, which at the 3 % EMA cap takes 50–70 observations to correct downward. A `COLD_START_SOLAR_DAMPING = 0.5` factor is applied to both the 2D and 1D collinear jump-start paths so EMA converges from a conservative underestimate rather than correcting an overshoot. The damping value is a named constant and can be tuned as more real-world data is collected.
- Lowered aux cooldown convergence threshold from 95% to 92%. Radiant heat sources (e.g. wood stoves) warm walls and furniture in ways that linger well beyond the active burn period; waiting for 95% normalisation was unnecessarily conservative. 92% exits the learning lock earlier while still preventing thermal-lag bias in the model.

## [1.2.1] - 2026-03-02

### Added
- Added `calibrate_inertia` service that analyses historical "pure" hours (no aux, no solar, learning active) and tests Gaussian kernels from 1–24 hours, returning R² and RMSE for each. The service returns the best overall profile, a top-5 ranking, a per-week stability breakdown, and a stability score. Intended to replace the arbitrary 4-hour default with a data-driven recommendation.
- Added `centered_energy_average` parameter to `calibrate_inertia` (default `false`). When enabled, actual energy consumption is smoothed with a 3-hour centred moving average before correlation is calculated, reducing noise from heat pump defrost cycles or partial-hour meter delays without shifting the identified inertia peak.
- Added `generate_gaussian_kernel(hours)` helper in `helpers.py`, shared between the coordinator's live inertia calculation and the calibration service.

### Changed
- Thermal inertia configuration replaced the fast / normal / slow dropdown with a numeric slider (1–24 h). The coordinator generates the Gaussian kernel dynamically from the chosen value. Existing configurations are migrated automatically: `fast → 2 h`, `normal → 4 h`, `slow → 12 h`.
- Removed `hourly` sub-section from the `forecast_accuracy_by_source` attribute on the Weather Plan Today sensor. The hourly temperature error metrics (`p50_abs_error`, `p95_abs_error`, `mae_7d`, etc.) are internal inputs to the source-blending logic and not meaningful to end users. Only the `daily` energy accuracy metrics are now exposed.

### Fixed
- Fixed `calibrate_inertia` incorrectly excluding hours logged with `learning_status: "active"`. Only `"success"` and `"model_updated"` were accepted, discarding the majority of normal operating hours.
- Fixed a restart-at-hourly-boundary race condition where `_last_hour_processed` was not persisted to storage. After a restart or config reload at the top of an hour the coordinator re-initialised the tracker to the current hour, silently skipping `_process_hourly_data` for the missed hour and leaving a gap in `_hourly_log`. The gap appeared as fewer inertia samples than the configured profile width (e.g. 3 samples instead of 4). A duplicate-entry guard is also added to `_process_hourly_data` as a safe fallback if the crash occurred after the log entry was already appended.
- Fixed "Deviation Today" percentage drifting with weather forecast revisions. The numerator now compares `forecast_today_gross` against `predicted_gross` — both in the aux-unaware (gross) domain — so live forecast updates cancel out in the difference and only genuine consumption deviations are surfaced. The denominator remains the frozen `midnight_forecast` to prevent the percentage from scaling with cold-weather revisions mid-day.

### Docs
- Updated DESIGN.md section 3B–3C with precise definitions of all per-source accuracy metrics exposed in `forecast_accuracy_by_source`: `mae_Xd`/`mape_Xd` (net daily energy error, kWh/day), `weather_mae_Xd` (mean absolute net daily temperature deviation, °C), and `weather_bias_Xd` (signed mean net daily temperature deviation, °C). Clarifies sign convention, why net daily error is used over sum of hourly absolutes, and that temperature metrics serve as internal blend-selection inputs rather than end-user KPIs.
