## [1.3.1] - 2026-04-17

### Added
- **`diagnose_solar` service.** New diagnostic service for per-unit solar coefficient validation and global solar model health. Reports implied coefficients (back-calculated via normal equations), coefficient stability across 3 time windows, saturation frequency, temporal bias (morning vs afternoon), battery decay health (post-sunset residual analysis), screen correction impact (stratified error comparison), and hour-of-day residual curve. Includes battery decay calibration: sweeps decay rates 0.50–0.95 and recommends the optimal value. Set `apply_battery_decay: true` to persist the recommendation. Single-pass over hourly log, same pattern as `diagnose_model`.

### Changed
- **Solar coefficient learning switched from LMS to NLMS.** The standard LMS gradient descent had gain-dependent instability: high-solar units (e.g. large south-facing windows) received updates proportional to solar vector magnitude squared, causing oscillation. NLMS (Normalized LMS) divides the step by input power, making convergence rate independent of solar magnitude. The epsilon regularization parameter (0.05) naturally shrinks the east coefficient when the east signal is weak (typical at 60°N). Replaces the `PER_UNIT_LEARNING_RATE_CAP` for solar learning (kept for base/aux).
- **Solar coefficients learned against potential (pre-screen) solar vector.** Previously, per-unit solar coefficients were learned against the effective (post-screen) vector, coupling screen state into the coefficient. Screen automation caused coefficient drift — windowless rooms accumulated phantom coefficients as the global correction oscillated. Coefficients now learn against the potential (pre-screen) vector and implicitly absorb the average screen transmittance: `impact = coefficient × potential_vector`. Both the prediction and normalization paths use the same formula — no separate screen transmittance factor is applied. Existing coefficients will naturally re-converge within 1-2 weeks of sunny hours.
- **Cloud coverage exponent corrected from 1.5 to 3.4 (Kasten & Czeplak 1980).** The previous exponent was introduced in v1.3.0 as an approximation of the Kasten formula but was incorrectly transcribed — the published value is 3.4. The difference is significant at partial cloud coverage: at 50% cloud the old exponent reduced solar by 27% while Kasten gives 7%. The error was not constant across cloud levels (2% at clear sky, 39% at 70% cloud), forcing the per-unit coefficient to absorb a cloud-level-dependent bias instead of representing stable window physics. With exponent 3.4, the bias is nearly constant (~1%) across all cloud levels, making the coefficient portable across weather providers and stable across seasons. Existing coefficients are inflated by the absorbed cloud bias (~1.2–1.4×) and will re-converge via NLMS within 10–20 qualifying solar hours.
- **Solar battery decay default raised from 0.60 to 0.75.** The previous half-life of 1.4 hours only captured light thermal mass (furniture, interior surfaces). The new half-life of ~2.4 hours is a middle ground that works for both lightweight (furniture, interior surfaces) and heavier (concrete, floor heating) construction without over-crediting residual solar in the evening hours. Per-installation calibration is available via `diagnose_solar` service with `apply_battery_decay: true`. The decay rate is stored in `entry.data` and can be overridden without config flow changes.
- **Per-unit mode selectors always created.** Mode select entities (heating/cooling/off/dhw/guest) are now created for every energy sensor regardless of the `has_ac_units` toggle. Previously, users without AC could not set DHW or Guest modes — critical for correct mode filtering of hot water energy and visitor impact. The `has_ac_units` toggle has been removed from the config flow. Existing configurations are unaffected; the toggle value in `entry.data` is simply ignored.
- **Physics config step simplified.** Removed `has_ac_units` toggle and cleaned up stale field references (wind sensors and indoor temp sensor labels that had already moved to the feature config page).

### Fixed
- **`_validate_physics` dead wind sensor check removed.** The physics step validated `wind_speed_sensor` presence to skip weather entity wind checks, but the field was moved to the feature_config page in v1.3.0. The conditional was always false, making the validation effectively correct (always checks weather entity) but misleading. Now unconditionally validates that the weather entity supplies `wind_speed`.
- **Screen transmittance formula consolidated (DRY).** The `MIN + (1 − MIN) × correction/100` formula was duplicated in five locations across `learning.py`, `statistics.py`, and `coordinator.py` (`diagnose_solar`). All now delegate to `SolarCalculator._screen_transmittance()`, eliminating the risk of the formula diverging between the learning, prediction, and diagnostic paths.
- **Spurious warnings suppressed during HA startup (#813).** Six warnings in the coordinator (wind speed unavailable, cloud coverage not numeric, sun.sun entity missing, humidity missing, energy meter reset, energy spike) fired during the first update cycle before HA entities were available, polluting the log on every restart. All now check `hass.is_running` before logging, matching the existing pattern used by the outdoor temperature and wind sensor warnings. Protective logic (default values, reading skips) is unchanged — only the log output is suppressed during the startup window.
- **Solar prediction and normalization double-counted screen transmittance.** The per-unit coefficient absorbs screen transmittance implicitly via NLMS learning (coeff ≈ phys × trans). The prediction and normalization paths applied screen transmittance a second time, producing `phys × trans² × potential` instead of the correct `phys × trans × potential`. At `correction_percent = 50` this underestimated solar impact by ~40%; at 20% by ~80%. Fixed by removing the explicit `screen_transmittance` argument from all three consumption paths (normalization, prediction, diagnostics). No impact at `correction_percent = 100` (screens fully open). The coefficient continuously tracks the current screen state via NLMS; transient errors after screen changes are self-correcting within ~10 qualifying hours.
- **Screen correction averaged over the hour instead of end-of-hour snapshot.** `correction_percent` in the hourly observation was previously read once at the hour boundary. When screen automation changed the correction mid-hour, the snapshot could be inconsistent with the accumulated `solar_factor` and `solar_vector` (which correctly reflected the screen position at each 2-minute sample). The `ObservationCollector` now accumulates `correction_percent` at every sample and uses the hourly average, consistent with temperature, wind, and solar accumulation.

## [1.3.0] - 2026-04-07

### Added
- **Thermodynamic Baseline Engine (Track C).** New optional learning mode that decouples the model from MPC load-shifting. At midnight, Heating Analytics fetches 24 hours of delivered thermal energy from a connected `heatpump_mpc` integration and redistributes it across hours proportionally to weather-driven heat loss (ΔT × wind × solar⁻¹). The synthetic baseline is converted to electrical via per-hour COP (see below), retaining Track A's hourly bucket resolution while eliminating the feedback loop where the model learns MPC scheduling decisions as building physics. DHW hours receive their proportional thermal share since the building envelope still loses heat. Falls back to Track B thermal mass correction when MPC is unavailable.
- **Per-hour COP from MPC model parameters.** Track C now divides each hour's smeared thermal load by that hour's actual COP (computed from MPC's learned η_Carnot, leaving water temperature, and a defrost penalty for cold+humid conditions) rather than using a single daily average. This correctly attributes cold hours (low COP → high electrical cost) separately from mild hours (high COP → low electrical cost). COP is capped at 10.0 to prevent physically unrealistic values when outdoor temperature approaches LWT. The 24-hour synthetic total is renormalized to match actual metered consumption, preserving the per-hour shape while anchoring the daily sum to reality. When MPC parameters are unavailable, falls back to daily average COP (where COP cancels mathematically).
- **Humidity collection.** Relative humidity is now collected from the HA weather entity every minute, averaged per hour, and stored in the hourly log. Used by the per-hour COP defrost penalty model (icing conditions require both cold temperature AND high humidity). When the weather entity does not provide humidity, a conservative default of 50% is used for COP calculations.
- **Track C config flow integration.** A `Thermodynamic Baseline (Track C)` toggle appears in Advanced Options when Daily Learning Mode is active. Requires an `MPC Managed Sensor` field to identify which energy sensor is MPC-controlled; all other sensors contribute meter data directly.
- **Track C global model includes non-MPC units.** In multi-unit installations, the midnight learning loop adds non-MPC units' actual hourly kWh from the hourly log to each hour's synthetic value before writing to the correlation table. Daily history stores `track_c_kwh` (inclusive total), `track_c_kwh_mpc_only`, and `track_c_kwh_non_mpc` for diagnostics.
- **`retrain_from_history` Track C path.** Replays stored `track_c_distribution` through the per-hour buffer → jump-start → EMA pipeline. Days without distribution fall back to Track B. Also rebuilds non-MPC per-unit models to support `isolate_sensor` subtraction after reset.
- **Subtraction-based forecast isolation (`isolate_sensor`).** The `get_forecast` service accepts an optional `isolate_sensor` parameter returning `max(0, global − Σ other units)` per hour, preventing MPC double-accounting.
- **`retrain_from_history` COP-weighted smearing (experimental).** New `experimental_cop_smear` parameter for Track B installations with MPC COP parameters available. Distributes daily energy across 24 hours using per-hour COP weights instead of flat `q/24`, producing up to 24 temperature buckets per day. Available as a retrain option only — not used in daily live learning.

### Changed
- **Unified global model with cooling support.** The global correlation model now includes cooling-mode energy instead of discarding it. A saturation-aware solar normalization delta — computed per-unit from `calculate_total_power`'s mode-signed, saturation-clamped solar breakdown — replaces the previous temperature-based `global_mode` inference that applied a single solar direction to the aggregate. Buildings running heating and cooling simultaneously (e.g. floor cables + AC at different setpoints) are now correctly normalized: heating solar is added back, cooling solar is subtracted, using the same saturated values as the prediction path. The `solar_normalization_delta` is persisted in both the hourly log and daily history vectors for long-term reconstruction.
- **Track B/C daily learning supports cooling regime.** The strategy dispatch, Track B flat daily, and Track B COP-smearing paths now apply `solar_normalization_delta` from the hourly log when writing to the correlation table. Loss weights use `abs(ΔT)` instead of `max(0, BP−T)`, giving cooling hours proportional weight. Solar multiplier is inverted for cooling hours (sun increases demand). The ThermodynamicEngine's `_calculate_theoretical_loss_weight` accepts an `is_cooling` flag for correct solar direction in Track C smearing.
- **`heating_total_kwh` and `cooling_total_kwh` in prediction breakdown.** `calculate_total_power` now returns per-mode net unit sums and per-mode saturated solar fields (`solar_heating_applied_kwh`, `solar_cooling_applied_kwh`), making the heating/cooling composition transparent to downstream consumers.
- **Config flow decluttered.** Wind tuning parameters (gust factor, high/extreme wind thresholds) moved from the Advanced Options page to the Feature Settings page, shown only when the dedicated wind sensor toggle is enabled. CSV logging and spike protection threshold moved to the bottom of Advanced Options. The onboarding flow now presents toggles and feature selection first, with technical tuning deferred to the feature-specific page.
- **Cloud coverage model upgraded to Kasten-derived power law.** The solar model's cloud factor changed from linear (`1 − cloud/100`) to `1 − 0.75 × (cloud/100)^1.5`. The linear model overstated solar reduction at partial coverage (30–70%); the power law better matches empirical data — thin/partial clouds transmit more solar energy than a linear interpolation predicts. Applied in both `calculate_solar_factor` and `calculate_solar_vector`. The per-unit solar coefficient learning absorbs any systematic shift during the transition; no user action needed.
- **MPC sensor excluded from per-unit Track A learning.** Load-shifted electrical data from the MPC-managed sensor is filtered from the `energy_sensors` list passed to `process_learning()` when Track C is active.
- **Config flow decluttered.** Wind sensor fields moved behind a "Use Dedicated Wind Sensor" toggle (off by default). Wind tuning (thresholds, gust factor) moved to the feature config page but remains always visible — relevant regardless of wind source and needed by Track B/C users who cannot use `calibrate_wind_thresholds`. Weather entity wind data is recommended for most installations.

### Internal
- **Coordinator decomposition (#775, Phases 1–4).** Introduces `HourlyObservation` (frozen dataclass), `ModelState`, `LearningConfig`, and `ObservationCollector` as explicit data contracts. Twenty mutable hour-scoped accumulators consolidated into `ObservationCollector` with atomic `reset()`. `LearningManager.process_learning()` reduced from ~30 parameters to three typed objects. Cached `coordinator.model` property replaces 64 direct private-field accesses across statistics, forecast, solar, and sensor modules. Storage remains the canonical write-path. No behavioral change.
- **Per-unit learning strategy pattern (#776).** Replaces the growing if/else tree in `_process_daily_data` with a `LearningStrategy` protocol and two implementations: `DirectMeter` (returns actual per-hour kWh from meter) and `WeightedSmear` (returns COP-corrected synthetic kWh from Track C distribution). `build_strategies()` factory auto-assigns from existing config — Track C MPC sensor gets `WeightedSmear`, all others get `DirectMeter`. `_apply_strategies_to_global_model()` iterates strategies uniformly. `retrain_from_history` uses the same dispatch. Track B flattened daily learning (`q_adjusted/24` to one bucket) is preserved as a separate path — strategy dispatch is only used when Track C distribution is available.

### Fixed
- **Per-unit solar coefficients clamped to ≥ 0.** Prevents physically impossible negative coefficients from confounding variables (e.g. scheduled pre-heating coinciding with morning sun).
- **`reset_learning_data` now clears all learned state.** Previously left `_aux_coefficients`, `_learning_buffer_global`, and `_learned_u_coefficient` untouched, silently biasing predictions after reset.
- **`retrain_from_history` `reset_first` scope aligned with `reset_learning_data`.** The inline reset now clears the same fields, preventing stale per-unit state from blending with fresh learning. Uses in-place `.clear()` to preserve internal references.
- **`retrain_from_history` no longer produces 0 days under Daily Learning Mode.** The poison filter now accepts `"disabled"` entries (Track A skip marker) when operating in daily mode.
- **`async_restore_data` now restores global `_aux_coefficients`.** Previously lost after a backup/restore cycle despite being saved.
- **Model cache invalidated after reset and retrain operations.** `_invalidate_model_cache()` now called in `reset_learning_data`, `retrain_from_history`, and `restore_data` to prevent stale `learned_u_coefficient` or correlation data references.
- **Solar coefficient clamping enforced on all storage load paths.** Previously, negative `{s, e}` components could enter the live dict from pre-clamp storage files, old-format migration (where `-cos(azimuth)` decomposition produces negative `s`), or backup restore. All three paths now apply `max(0.0, ...)` via a shared `_sanitize_solar_coeff()` helper, matching the clamping already enforced in the learning path.
- **Humidity averaging uses dedicated counter.** When the weather entity does not provide humidity, `humidity_sum` stayed at 0.0 while `sample_count` was 60, producing `avg_humidity = 0.0` instead of `None`. At midnight sync, `0.0` passed the `is not None` check and disabled the defrost penalty entirely. Now uses a separate `humidity_count` — `None` is returned when no humidity data was received, correctly triggering the 50% default fallback.
- **Track B/C daily learning now filters excluded modes (#789).** Energy from units in OFF, DHW, and Guest modes is excluded from the daily learning paths (Track B flat bucket and Track C strategy dispatch), matching Track A's hourly filtering semantics. Previously, OFF/DHW/Guest energy leaked into the daily total, corrupting the global model. The mode filter is applied per-hour from the `unit_modes` snapshot in the hourly log. `DirectMeter` and `WeightedSmear` strategies also check unit modes and return `None` for excluded modes. `retrain_from_history` applies the same filter. Cooling mode is no longer excluded — it participates in the global model with correct solar normalization (see Changed section).
- **`replace_sensor_source` now supports multi-instance targeting.** Added optional `entity_id` parameter to the service call, consistent with other services. Without it, the legacy behavior (try all instances) is preserved.
- **`get_forecast` now validates input.** Added server-side schema validation (entity_id, days 1–14, isolate_sensor). Previously accepted any data without validation. MPC contract unchanged.
- **`reset_solar_learning` now targets a single instance.** Previously iterated all coordinators regardless of `entity_id`. Now uses `_get_target_coordinator` for instance targeting, with a separate `unit_entity_id` parameter for per-unit filtering. Added missing `services.yaml` definition.
- **`_get_target_coordinator` no longer silently falls back on failed explicit lookup.** When `entity_id` is provided but the entity registry lookup fails, a `ValueError` is raised instead of silently targeting an arbitrary instance. The first-available fallback is preserved when `entity_id` is omitted.
- **Hourly warning when weather entity lacks numeric `cloud_coverage`.** The condition-text fallback (e.g. "sunny" → 10%) is too coarse for reliable solar coefficient learning. A rate-limited warning now appears in the log once per hour, recommending Met.no or the custom Open-Meteo fork.
- **Forecast `cloud_coverage` priority inverted.** `forecast.py` checked condition-text (e.g. "partlycloudy" → 50%) *before* checking for a numeric `cloud_coverage` attribute — the opposite of what the coordinator does. All forecast predictions used the coarse mapping even when the weather provider supplied precise data. Fixed in three locations (hourly reference map, per-item processing, daily forecast).
- **Hourly warning when wind speed data is unavailable.** When neither the weather entity nor a dedicated sensor provides wind speed, all hours land in the `normal` bucket, disabling wind differentiation. A rate-limited warning now alerts the user.
- **Hourly warning when `sun.sun` entity is unavailable.** When the Sun integration is missing or temporarily down, the entire solar model produces zero — silently corrupting the base thermal model. A rate-limited warning now alerts the user.
- **Hourly warning when humidity is unavailable (Track C).** When the weather entity lacks humidity data, the COP defrost penalty uses a 50% default which may be inaccurate. Warning is shown only when Track C is active.

## [1.2.9] - 2026-03-27

### Added
- **Solar thermal battery.** Introduces an exponential decay accumulator (`_solar_battery_state`) that charges on each hour's raw solar impact and decays at a fixed rate (`SOLAR_BATTERY_DECAY = 0.60` per hour, ~20 % remaining after 3 hours). This models heat absorbed by the building mass during peak sun being released gradually over the following hours, rather than treating solar gain as an instantaneous per-hour subtraction. The smoothed `effective_solar_impact` replaces the raw value in the learning normalisation call (`normalize_for_learning`), the thermodynamic gross calculation, and the hourly log entry (`solar_impact_kwh`). The raw instantaneous value is preserved in the live `solar_impact_kwh` sensor attribute and the dual-interference guard (which should only trigger during active sun), and is additionally logged as `solar_impact_raw_kwh` for debugging. The battery state is now persisted across restarts (see below); no configuration or calibration is required.
- **Solar battery state persisted across restarts.** `_solar_battery_state` is now saved to both the primary and backup storage files and restored on load (defaulting to `0.0` when absent). Previously a restart would silently zero the battery, causing the first post-restart sunny hour to understate effective solar impact — the base model would then absorb the residual gain as a thermal bias and drive the solar coefficients toward a stable but systematically low equilibrium.

### Changed
- **`compare_periods` now tracks the data basis of each period explicitly.** Each period in the result carries a `basis` field (`"actual"` / `"modeled"` / `"hybrid"`) describing what the energy figures actually represent: `"actual"` means all days are past with real consumption measurements, `"modeled"` means no consumption was recorded (system not tracking that year), and `"hybrid"` means the period spans today or future (mix of actuals and forecast). The basis is also surfaced as top-level `period_1_basis` / `period_2_basis` fields for easy automation access. Downstream text is now basis-aware: the `Summary` attribute says **"vs last year's model"** (not "vs last year") when the reference period has no measurements, and the `Comparison Summary` label follows the same logic. `delta_actual_kwh` is set to `None` when either period lacks real measurements, preventing the misleading `2030 − 0 = 2030` delta that previously appeared when the reference year had no energy tracking.
- **`COLD_START_SOLAR_DAMPING` raised from `0.5` to `0.75`.** The cold-start jump-start estimate is computed while the base model is still in early learning, so some dampening is warranted to guard against noise-inflated outliers. However, a 50 % cut proved too aggressive: starting at half the true value and climbing via a 3 %/hour EMA cap on a sparse set of clear-sky, aux-free hours meant the coefficient could take months to converge — if it converged at all. At 0.75 the initial estimate is 25 % below the least-squares fit, converges within roughly 10 qualifying solar hours, and is still protected against early-learning overestimates.
- **CSV logging path fields are now hidden behind the `csv_auto_logging` toggle in the setup/reconfigure wizard.** The hourly and daily file path fields only appear when CSV logging is enabled, keeping the advanced step uncluttered for users who do not use this feature. Default paths are pre-filled (`/config/heating_analytics_hourly_log.csv` and `/config/heating_analytics_daily_log.csv`) so enabling the feature requires no extra configuration unless a custom path is needed.

## [1.2.8] - 2026-03-20

### Changed
- **`calibrate_wind_thresholds` response aligned with `calibrate_inertia` quality.** Five new fields have been added to the return object to bring it to the same information density as the inertia service:
  - **`discarded_hours`** — dict with a per-reason count of filtered-out hours (`zero_or_negative_consumption`, `solar_interference`, `auxiliary_active`, `missing_wind_or_temp`, `total_discarded`). Previously only `pure_hours_found` was available, making it impossible to diagnose why data was missing.
  - **`current_distribution` / `recommended_distribution`** — hour count per bucket (`normal`, `high_wind`, `extreme_wind`) for the current and recommended threshold pair respectively. Critical for judging whether enough wind observations exist for the recommendation to be statistically meaningful.
  - **`recommended_per_bucket_mae`** — MAE broken down per bucket for the recommended pair. Aggregate MAE can hide a poorly-fitted `extreme_wind` bucket when that bucket contains few hours.
  - **`improvement_pct`** — MAE improvement from the current to the recommended threshold pair, expressed as a percentage (`(current_mae − recommended_mae) / current_mae × 100`), making it easy to judge whether the change is worth applying.
  - **`data_quality`** — human-readable string replacing the binary `insufficient_windy_hours` flag: `"High (N windy hours)"` at ≥ 100 hours, `"Medium (N windy hours — borderline reliable)"` at 30–99, `"Low (N windy hours — treat as advisory, more wind data needed)"` below 30.

- **Overnight load shift correction moved and gated in the setup wizard.** The majority of support questions about thermal mass correction ("am I calculating this correctly?") come from users who are not actually load-shifting energy across midnight — the correction does not apply to them. The config flow has been restructured to make this clear:
  - **`indoor_temp_sensor` removed from step 2** (physics). It had no semantic connection to the other physics fields; its only purpose is thermal mass correction.
  - **New `Overnight load shift correction` toggle added in step 3**, nested under `Daily Learning Mode`. It is off by default and shown only when Track B is active. Enabling it reveals `indoor_temp_sensor` and `thermal_mass_kwh_per_degree`. The toggle is UI-only and is not stored in `entry.data`; on reconfigure its default state is derived from whether `indoor_temp_sensor` is already configured.
  - The coupling is now explicit: load shift correction requires Track B (daily learning). Users running Track B for high-thermal-mass or modulation-floor reasons — who do not load-shift — are no longer prompted to configure fields that do not apply to them.

## [1.2.7] - 2026-03-13

### Added
- **Daily Learning Mode (Track B).** A new optional config flag (`Daily Learning Mode`) switches the model from hourly learning (Track A) to a single nightly update at midnight. Designed for homes where hourly observations are unreliable: high thermal mass buildings (concrete, stone, passive house) and heat pumps with a high minimum modulation floor. When enabled, Track A is completely blocked from writing to the correlation table — Track B owns it exclusively. The daily bucket EMA uses the user's configured learning rate, consistent with Track A behaviour. Requires at least 22 of 24 hours to be present in the day log before any update is applied (raised from 20). An optional **Indoor Temperature Sensor** can be added to apply thermal mass correction before learning (`q_adjusted = daily_kWh − thermal_mass_factor × ΔT_indoor`); the sensor is not required — users who benefit from daily aggregation without load-shifting can leave it empty. A new **Daily Learning diagnostic sensor** (`heating_analytics_daily_learning`) is registered automatically when the mode is active, exposing the learned U-coefficient (kWh/TDD) as the state value and `last_midnight_indoor_temp`, `thermal_mass_kwh_per_degree`, `indoor_temp_sensor`, and `learning_rate` as attributes.

- **`retrain_from_history` service.** Retrains the learning model directly from the existing 90-day hourly log — no CSV file needed. In Track A mode (standard hourly learning) each logged hour is replayed through the same per-hour EMA path used during live operation, honouring aux/base routing. In Track B mode (Daily Learning Mode) hours are grouped by calendar day and the nightly batch EMA is applied; thermal mass correction is applied automatically using midnight indoor temperatures stored in `daily_history` (see below). Accepts optional `days_back` (limit to the most recent N days) and `reset_first` (wipe the existing model before retraining for a full cold-start rebuild). Returns a response with `mode`, `entries_processed`, `days_processed`, `learning_count`, and — for Track B — the updated `learned_u_coefficient`.
- **CSV import now correctly handles Daily Learning Mode (Track B).** Previously, calling `import_from_csv` with `update_model: true` while `daily_learning_mode` was active would silently run per-hour Track A learning against the Track B–owned correlation table, potentially corrupting the nightly calibration. The import now detects the active mode and routes accordingly: Track A is used when daily learning is off (unchanged behaviour); Track B batch aggregation is used when it is on. Imported hours are grouped by calendar day — days with fewer than 22 hours are skipped — and the same midnight EMA logic is applied (TDD ≥ 0.5, U-coefficient EMA, per–temp/wind-bucket EMA). Thermal mass correction is intentionally omitted for CSV imports because historical indoor temperatures are not available in the file.
- **Midnight indoor temperature stored per day in `daily_history`.** When `daily_learning_mode` is active and an Indoor Temperature Sensor is configured, the sensor value read at midnight is now persisted as `midnight_indoor_temp` on the `daily_history` entry for that day (in addition to updating `_last_midnight_indoor_temp` as before). This allows `retrain_from_history` to reconstruct the per-day ΔT (`midnight_indoor_temp[D] − midnight_indoor_temp[D−1]`) and apply the same thermal mass correction historically as the live nightly calibration does in real time. Days without a stored value (data predating this version) are reprocessed without correction, which is safe — it matches the previous behaviour.

- **Setup wizard.** The integration setup and reconfigure flows have been redesigned as a three-step wizard. Step 1 collects the essentials (name, weather provider, energy meters, outdoor temperature sensor). Step 2 covers sensor overrides and building physics (wind sensors, balance point, thermal inertia, AC mode). Step 3 exposes advanced options (learning mode, wind thresholds, aux entities, CSV logging, etc.). The source-selector dropdowns for outdoor temperature and wind have been removed — a sensor field left blank automatically falls back to the weather provider. Solar gain tracking is now enabled by default for all new and reconfigured instances.
- **Sensor swap guard in reconfigure.** Simultaneously removing one energy sensor and adding another in the reconfigure wizard is almost always an attempt to replace a renamed or broken sensor inline, which silently destroys the learned auxiliary coefficients for the removed unit. The wizard now detects this pattern and blocks progression with an error directing the user to the `replace_sensor` service call, which performs the substitution without data loss. Pure additions and pure removals are unaffected.

### Changed
- **Learning Rate removed from config flow.** The setting is now only adjustable via the `number.learning_rate` entity in HA, which persists the value across restarts. The config flow field was redundant — storage always overwrites the config entry value after first run. The default remains 1%.

### Fixed
- **`midnight_forecast_effective_wind_secondary` shows `0` instead of `null` when secondary weather source lacks wind data.** The sensor attribute for secondary-source forecast wind was using `item.get("wind_speed", 0.0)` as fallback, silently treating a missing `wind_speed` key as calm conditions (0 m/s). Weather integrations that do not include `wind_speed` in their hourly forecast payloads (e.g. certain third-party providers) would therefore always report `0` for this attribute regardless of actual conditions. The attribute now returns `null` when `wind_speed` is absent. Additionally, the wind unit was always read from the primary weather entity, causing incorrect unit conversion when the secondary entity reports wind speed in a different unit (e.g. primary in m/s, secondary in km/h). Each source now resolves its own `wind_speed_unit` from the respective weather entity's state attributes.
- **Multi-instance service targeting.** Six service calls (`import_from_csv`, `export_to_csv`, `backup_data`, `restore_data`, `compare_periods`, `exit_cooldown`) previously executed against all active Heating Analytics instances with no way to target a specific one. In a single-instance setup this was harmless, but with multiple instances it caused the wrong instance's data to be exported or backed up, the same CSV or backup file to be imported or restored into every instance simultaneously, and period comparisons to silently target an unintended coordinator. All six services now accept an optional `entity_id` field (any sensor belonging to the desired instance) and route to the correct coordinator. The fallback behaviour — first available instance — is unchanged when `entity_id` is omitted.
- DHW energy consumption is now fully excluded from heating totals. Previously, energy consumed during DHW cycles was still accumulating in _accumulated_energy_hour, _accumulated_energy_today, _hourly_delta_per_unit, and _daily_individual — even though the learning model correctly treated the heat contribution as zero. This resulted in inflated daily totals, incorrect thermodynamic variance, and skewed forecasts during DHW-heavy periods. The exclusion logic is now applied during the 2-minute accumulation tick (evaluating the state 30 times per hour), ensuring that mid-hour mode switches are tracked accurately. _last_energy_values continues to update independently of the current mode, guaranteeing a correct delta baseline when the unit switches back to heating.

## [1.2.6] - 2026-03-08

### Added
- **DHW mode support for air-to-water heat pumps.** Units that periodically switch to domestic hot water production now have a dedicated `MODE_DHW` state. During a DHW cycle the heat pump delivers zero heat to the space; the model correctly observes this as zero contribution and learns accordingly. The previous behaviour treated DHW like `MODE_OFF` (skipping the learning update entirely), which caused systematic upward drift in the learned coefficient proportional to the DHW duty cycle — equivalent to a heating-cable hysteresis error. Setting a unit to `MODE_DHW` eliminates the drift at all learning rates. At the default learning rate (1%) the steady-state oscillation amplitude stays within ±7% of the true coefficient even in extreme DHW-heavy operating profiles.
- **Blueprints for automatic mode state mapping** are now included in the repository under `blueprints/`. `heat_pump_mode_sync.yaml` maps a heat pump's operation mode sensor (Heating / Domestic Hot Water / Defrost) to the Heating Analytics mode select helper automatically — Defrost is handled transparently by preserving the current mode so defrost energy is attributed to the active heating or DHW cycle. `climate_sync.yaml` maps standard climate entity states (heat / cool / off) to the mode helper, with an optional guest mode prefix for tracking occupancy separately.
- **Solar screen transmittance floor (`MIN_SOLAR_TRANSMITTANCE = 0.20`).** The previous screen correction applied a pure linear scale (`effective = correction_percent / 100`), collapsing the effective solar vector to zero when all screens were closed. This suppressed solar-coefficient learning (the `vector_magnitude ≤ 0.01` guard in the learning engine returns early when the vector is near-zero) and forced the base thermal model to silently absorb residual solar gain as a systematic downward bias in temperature-bucket coefficients. Physically, a closed screen still transmits ~10–15 % of solar irradiance through its fabric (G-value/SHGC), and unmonitored windows without screens contribute additional baseline gain. The new formula `effective = MIN + (1 − MIN) × (correction_percent / 100)` ensures the effective vector stays non-zero at all screen positions. At 0 % open: effective = 0.20; at 100 % open: effective = 1.0 (unchanged). Existing learned solar coefficients were calibrated against the old scale and will converge to corrected values via EMA (lr = 0.01) over a few weeks of sunny weather — no manual reset required.

### Fixed
- **Multiple instances share the same storage file.** The storage key was the fixed string `heating_analytics.storage`, causing all instances on the same HA installation to read and write the same file — each restart overwrote the other's learned model with its own data. Each `StorageManager` instance now uses a per-entry key (`heating_analytics.storage_<entry_id>`). On first load with the new code, a one-time migration from the legacy shared file is attempted only if the stored `daily_history` contains at least one sensor that is also configured for the instance — a mismatch means the stored data belongs to a different instance, so that instance starts fresh instead. Existing single-instance installations migrate their learned data automatically; new or secondary instances start with a clean slate.
- **Config flow blocks submission when switching source to "Weather Entity" ("Ikke alle obligatoriske felt er fylt ut").** Four root causes: (1) HA's frontend validates `vol.Required` fields *before* submitting to Python. When the user switched the source dropdown from *Dedicated Sensor* to *Weather Entity*, the now-irrelevant sensor `EntitySelector` remained visible on the form (config flow does not re-render on dropdown change) and its `vol.Required` marker blocked submission entirely — the backend reload logic never ran. Fixed by declaring outdoor temperature and wind speed sensor fields as `vol.Optional` in the schema; required validation for `SOURCE_SENSOR` mode is enforced in `_validate_weather_fallback` instead. A backend reload condition is also added so the form re-renders on the next submit to hide the unneeded field. (2) `wind_speed_sensor` had no backend required-validation when `SOURCE_SENSOR` was selected; the check existed for `outdoor_temp_sensor` but not for wind speed. Added the symmetric check. (3) Switching back to *Dedicated Sensor* after a prior *Weather Entity* save showed "Entity None is not a valid entity ID" because the sensor key was stored as `None`. Fixed by omitting the `default` kwarg when the stored value is falsy. (4) The `None` value also lingered in `entry.data` when HA/voluptuous silently dropped an absent `Optional` key on a re-save. Fixed by popping all four optional entity keys (`outdoor_temp_sensor`, `wind_speed_sensor`, `wind_gust_sensor`, `secondary_weather_entity`) from `new_data` when absent or falsy, rather than only handling `secondary_weather_entity`.

## [1.2.4] - 2026-03-06

### Added
- Added `test_asymmetric` flag to `calibrate_inertia` service. When enabled, evaluates a regime-based asymmetric inertia model alongside the standard symmetric scan: slow profile (4 h) when outdoor temperature is falling (heat shedding), fast profile (2 h) when rising (heat gaining), and a stable 3 h profile otherwise. Returns comparative R², RMSE, delta_r2 vs best symmetric, and a regime breakdown (shedding/gaining/stable hour counts).
- Added `test_delta_t_scaling` flag to `calibrate_inertia` service. Bins pure hours by (balance_point − outdoor_temp) in 5 °C steps and finds the optimal exponential τ per bin (`window = min(5τ, 168)`), consistent with the coordinator's runtime model. Tests the hypothesis that thermal inertia scales with temperature differential: low ΔT (mild weather) → short τ, high ΔT (deep winter) → long τ. A monotonic `best_tau` trend across bins confirms the hypothesis and would support a future ΔT-adaptive inertia model.

### Fixed
- **Solar learning buffer is no longer partitioned by temperature bucket.** The per-unit solar coefficient (`c_s`, `c_e`) models window area, orientation, and shading — physical properties that do not depend on outdoor temperature. Partitioning the cold-start buffer by `temp_key` caused it to accumulate at most 1–2 samples per bucket before the temperature drifted to the next bucket, so `LEARNING_BUFFER_THRESHOLD = 4` was never reached in any single bucket and jump-start never fired. The buffer is now a single flat list per unit (pooling solar observations across all temperatures), and the learned coefficient is stored globally per unit rather than per `(unit, temp_key)`. The `calculate_unit_coefficient` lookup in `solar.py` is simplified accordingly. On first load, storage migration flattens existing temp-stratified buffers into the new format and picks the first valid entry from any existing temp-stratified coefficient store.
- **Thermal inertia kernel corrected from Gaussian to causal exponential decay.** The Gaussian kernel `exp(−½((t − μ)/σ)²)` places its peak weight at the midpoint of the lookback window (e.g. 2 hours ago for a 4-hour setting), not at the current observation. A building's thermal mass follows first-order (RC-circuit) dynamics: the indoor heat content decays toward the outdoor temperature at a rate governed by the time constant τ = C_a / UA, where C_a is the effective thermal capacitance and UA is the total heat loss coefficient. The correct weighting function is therefore a causal exponential decay `e^(−t/τ)`, which assigns maximum weight to the present state and monotonically less to older samples — physically equivalent to the impulse response of an RC low-pass filter. The Gaussian has no analogue in building thermodynamics and systematically biased the effective temperature toward historical observations, effectively overstating inertia and producing recommendations that were too conservative. Replaced with `generate_exponential_kernel(tau)` throughout.
- **Kernel window capped at 5τ hours (captures 99.3 % of cumulative weight).** Previously the exponential kernel used the full 168-hour default window regardless of τ. For τ = 4 h this meant `_get_inertia_parameters` requested 167 hours of history and weighted 99 % of that data at negligible values. Window is now `min(5τ, 168)`, consistent with the standard RC-circuit approximation where five time constants represent effective convergence to steady state.
- **Stale-data gap detection decoupled from kernel window size.** `_get_inertia_parameters` derived `max_gap` from `len(inertia_weights)`, which after the window cap became 5τ instead of τ. This disabled the thermal-discontinuity guard: after a system restart or sensor outage, temperature logs from up to 20 hours ago (for τ = 4) were treated as continuous rather than discarded. `max_gap` is now `int(tau)`, preserving the original behaviour that only consecutive hourly data within one time constant is considered thermodynamically continuous.
- **`calibrate_inertia` now recommends τ directly applicable to the config-flow slider.** The service previously returned `best_overall.hours` from a Gaussian sweep. Because the coordinator uses an exponential kernel, the Gaussian recommendation was not directly comparable: the Gaussian peak at h = 6 compensated for its non-causal weighting by artificially widening the window, while the physical time constant is τ = 4 h. The primary output is now `recommended_tau` from an exponential sweep (τ = 1–24 h, `window = min(5τ, 168)`), matching the coordinator's runtime model exactly. The Gaussian sweep is retained as `gaussian_best_hours` / `gaussian_best_r2` for comparison. Weekly stability analysis and the optional extended sweep (`test_exponential_kernel`, τ = 1–72 h) are also converted to exponential.
- **Inertia weights attribute now shows normalised values (sum = 1.0).** The `weights` field on the Thermal State sensor previously exposed raw kernel fractions normalised over the full window (e.g. sum ≈ 0.64 when 10 of 45 kernel positions were active). The internal calculation already re-normalises within the active subset, but the displayed values did not reflect this, making manual verification (`Σ history[i] × weight[i] ≈ effective_temperature`) impossible without dividing by the displayed sum. Weights are now re-normalised to the active sample count before display.

### Changed
- **Cold-start solar coefficient estimates are now dampened by 50% before publishing.** The 4-sample least squares estimate used for jump-start is computed while the base model is itself in early learning, making `actual_impact` noisy. This systematically inflates the initial coefficient, which at the 3 % EMA cap takes 50–70 observations to correct downward. A `COLD_START_SOLAR_DAMPING = 0.5` factor is applied to both the 2D and 1D collinear jump-start paths so EMA converges from a conservative underestimate rather than correcting an overshoot. The damping value is a named constant and can be tuned as more real-world data is collected.
- Lowered aux cooldown convergence threshold from 95% to 92%. Radiant heat sources (e.g. wood stoves) warm walls and furniture in ways that linger well beyond the active burn period; waiting for 95% normalisation was unnecessarily conservative. 92% exits the learning lock earlier while still preventing thermal-lag bias in the model.
